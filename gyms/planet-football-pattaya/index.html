Overview

Planet Football is one of Pattaya's most established youth football academies — founded in 2009 specifically to provide children within the community a safe environment to play football, and grown into a full pyramid of development programs culminating in the FC Planet competitive club team that competes in weekly local leagues plus regional tournaments. With nearly 17 years of operation as of 2026, Planet Football has shaped a substantial part of Pattaya's youth football community.

The academy structure runs three weekday sessions between 17:00 and 19:00 for academy-level players, supplemented by weekend matches and tournaments. The pathway is structured: soccer schools (introductory) → development programme (committed) → academy (elite invite-only) → FC Planet club team (competitive). This multi-tier progression model mirrors European academy systems rather than the single-tier "anyone joins" structure of less serious football programs.

What distinguishes Planet Football beyond the structure is the dual focus on football skill plus life skills — the academy explicitly aims to "improve ability and how young footballers conduct themselves on the football pitch and in everyday life", with coaches helping to instil life skills into every player. For Pattaya families wanting football development that's both serious and character-building, Planet Football is one of the city's most credible options.

The 2009 Founding

The academy was established in 2009 with the explicit purpose of providing children within the community a safe environment to play football. The 2009 timing puts Planet Football among the early established youth football academies in Pattaya — predating most of the city's current academy ecosystem.

Key implications of the 17-year operating history:

  • Generations of players have come through the system
  • Older alumni now in their 20s — some progressed to college / semi-pro careers
  • Coaching continuity — long-term staff know the family base
  • Community relationships — schools, parents, regional federations
  • Established league participation — known quantity in Pattaya football scene

The Multi-Tier Development Pyramid

Planet Football operates a clear progression pathway:

1. Soccer Schools (Introductory)

  • Open enrollment for any interested child
  • Basic skill development — first touch, passing, shooting, dribbling
  • Game-fun balance — keeping kids engaged
  • Multi-age groups typical
  • Lower commitment — once or twice per week

2. Development Programme (Committed)

  • Step-up from soccer schools
  • Higher training frequency — likely 2-3 sessions per week
  • More tactical content — positions, team play, decision-making
  • Higher skill level expectation
  • Pathway to academy invitation

3. Academy (Elite, Invite-Only)

  • Invite-only based on ability and commitment
  • 3 weekday sessions 17:00-19:00
  • Advanced training program in safe environment
  • Direct path to FC Planet team consideration
  • Most players advance from soccer schools or development program

4. FC Planet (Competitive Team)

  • Club team representing the academy in competitive play
  • Weekly local league matches
  • Regional tournaments at different times of year
  • Vital game experience for players showing potential
  • Pathway possibility for players considering serious football careers

This four-tier structure is meaningfully more developed than single-tier youth football programs.

Character Development Focus

A distinctive Planet Football emphasis:

Beyond Football Skills

The academy explicitly aims to teach:

  • Conduct on the pitch — sportsmanship, respect for officials, teammate support
  • Conduct in everyday life — discipline, time management, integrity
  • Life skills via football — teamwork, leadership, perseverance, dealing with success and failure

Why This Matters

Many youth sports programs focus narrowly on skill/winning. Planet Football's explicit character emphasis:

  • Aligns with parental priorities for character-building beyond sport
  • Develops well-rounded young people rather than narrow athletes
  • Maintains values even for players who won't pursue professional football
  • Sustains long-term player engagement — kids who feel valued as people stay longer
