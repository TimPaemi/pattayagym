Kombat Group Thailand

Overview

Kombat Group is a multi-discipline all-inclusive training camp in the countryside east of Pattaya, designed around an immersive, residential model. It is consistently one of the highest-rated combat sports facilities on TripAdvisor for all of Thailand, frequently in the top three sports/fitness camps in the country. The camp's identity is built around the legacy of its founder, Christian Daghio, a 6-time Muay Thai world champion and WBA Asia / ABF boxing champion. The camp combines high-end training across multiple disciplines (Muay Thai, Western Boxing, MMA, BJJ, Krav Maga) with full-board accommodation, on-site Thai kitchen, daily massage, and a peaceful resort-style setting in the Huai Yai countryside.

This is not a walk-in-and-train type gym. Kombat Group is structured for week-plus immersive stays — you book a package, you live on-site, and your training, food, sleep, and recovery are all integrated.

History & Legacy

The camp was founded by Christian Daghio in 2005. Daghio was an Italian-born world-class fighter — multiple Muay Thai world titles, WBA Asia Light Heavyweight boxing title, and ABF boxing championship — who built Kombat Group as his vision of how training, hospitality, and sport should be combined.

Tragedy: Daghio suffered a knockdown in the 12th round of his WBC Asia Light Heavyweight title fight on October 26, 2018, fell into a coma, and passed away shortly after at age 49. His ethos — discipline, community, technical excellence, and care for guests — has remained the operating philosophy of the camp, which continues to operate under his legacy and is widely praised in 2025-2026 reviews as honoring his standards.

Disciplines

Unusually for a Pattaya camp, Kombat Group covers a full combat-sports buffet:

  • Muay Thai — flagship discipline, Daghio's specialty
  • Western Boxing — proper boxing program (rare in Pattaya)
  • MMA — cage and structured curriculum
  • Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu — full mat program
  • Krav Maga — self-defense focus
  • Weight loss / fitness program — combines martial arts, functional training, and individualized nutrition; popular with non-fighters

You can mix and match across disciplines during your stay, or focus on one.

Facilities

Reviews consistently call out the quality and condition of the facility:

  • 3 Muay Thai rings
  • 1 MMA cage
  • Many heavy bags, banana bags, uppercut bags
  • Free weights, kettlebells, TRX, jump ropes, medicine balls
  • Cardio machines
  • BJJ mat area
  • Outdoor pool
  • On-site kitchen with Thai chef (included in packages)
  • Massage room (daily massage included in some packages)
  • AC accommodation rooms / bungalows
  • Communal lounge / pool deck
  • Daily cleaning service

Accommodation

Bundled with the training package — typically AC rooms with bathroom, well-maintained. Reviewers describe rooms as "practical, clean, well-furnished." Guests note the comfortable communal spaces (pool deck, kitchen area) where students from around the world hang out together — strong "summer-camp-for-adults" social vibe.

Trainers

The trainer roster includes pro fighters across Muay Thai, boxing, BJJ, and MMA. Reviewers repeatedly highlight that coaches personally track your progress, scale intensity to your level, and care about technique — which is rare in tourist-heavy training markets.

Schedule

Two-a-days Mon-Sat with structured programming. Morning session typically focused on conditioning + technique; afternoon session on padwork, sparring, or specific discipline blocks depending on your track. Sundays are rest/optional. Fighters' track has additional ring time and sparring days.

Pricing

Sold as all-inclusive packages (training + accommodation + food + transfers in some cases). Indicative ranges:

  • 1 week all-inclusive: starting ~฿15,000-25,000
  • 2 weeks all-inclusive: ~฿25,000-40,000
  • 1 month all-inclusive: ~฿45,000-70,000
  • Drop-in / day-pass training: available but the camp is structured around residential bookings

Premium pricing reflects the all-in model — accommodation, three meals/day, daily massage, and unlimited training. Compare apples-to-apples vs. piecing it together separately.

Location & Getting There

  • Area: Huai Yai, East Pattaya — countryside setting, ~15-20 min by car from central Pattaya
  • Quiet, residential, peaceful — described as "a peaceful oasis"
  • Airport transfer commonly arranged for guests
  • Close enough to Pattaya for shopping/beach excursions on rest days

Pros

  • Multi-discipline — train Muay Thai, boxing, MMA, BJJ, Krav Maga in one place
  • All-inclusive package removes logistics
  • Top-tier trainer attention and feedback
  • Daily massage and recovery support
  • Excellent kitchen — Thai chef, healthy menu
  • Welcoming community vibe — guests from many countries
  • Quiet rural setting, away from Pattaya nightlife distraction
  • Languages: English, Italian, Spanish (Daghio's heritage), Thai

Cons

  • Premium price point — among the higher-tier options
  • Rural location — not for those wanting walking access to nightlife/beaches
  • Structured residential model — less flexible than drop-in gyms
  • A car/scooter or pre-booked transfers needed for most off-camp errands

Reputation Summary

Top-3 rated combat sports camp in all of Thailand on TripAdvisor. Particularly strong reputation among European clients (Daghio's Italian heritage created a strong European pipeline), beginners doing fitness/weight-loss programs, and serious amateurs doing fight prep camps. Word-of-mouth is exceptionally strong; high return-customer rate.

Best For

Travelers wanting the all-inclusive package experience • Beginners on transformation/weight-loss programs • European clients who appreciate the multi-language environment • Anyone wanting peace and structure away from Pattaya's chaos • Multi-discipline cross-training

Not Best For

Drop-in / casual training • Travelers wanting walkable Pattaya nightlife • Pure budget travelers • People who want a traditional Thai-only camp atmosphere
