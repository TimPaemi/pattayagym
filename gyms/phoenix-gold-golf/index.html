Overview

Phoenix Gold Golf & Country Club is a 27-hole resort-style championship golf facility located in the Huay Yai area south of Pattaya, designed by Dennis Griffiths and opened in 1993. The course is structured as three nine-hole loops — Mountain, Lakes, and Ocean — combinable into any 18-hole round, each named for the dominant terrain feature it traverses. The mix of wooded rolling terrain, gentle elevation changes, water hazards, and panoramic views of mountains and the Gulf of Thailand makes Phoenix Gold one of the most visually striking and beginner-friendly courses in the Pattaya region.

In the Pattaya golf hierarchy, Phoenix Gold sits comfortably in the upper-mid tier: less prestigious than Laem Chabang International (Jack Nicklaus design, World Golf Awards perennial nominee) or Burapha (Graham + Panks, Thailand Open host), but more accessible, more forgiving, and more visually varied than either. For mixed-ability golf groups and visitors wanting a beautiful course with reasonable challenge and weekday rates around ฿3,000, Phoenix Gold is one of the best choices in the region.

The Designer — Dennis Griffiths

Dennis Griffiths is an American golf course architect known for designs across the Southern United States, with several international projects including Phoenix Gold. His design philosophy emphasizes:

  • Natural terrain integration — using existing landforms rather than fighting them
  • Strategic variety — each hole presenting different shot demands
  • Visual appeal — vistas and aesthetic moments built into the routing
  • Playability across abilities — challenging from the back tees, reasonable from forward markers

Phoenix Gold reflects all four principles. The course works as both a resort experience for casual visitors and a strategic test for advanced players depending on tee selection.

The Three Nines

Phoenix Gold's defining feature is the 27-hole / three-loop arrangement, with each nine offering a distinct character. The course can be played as Mountain + Lakes (M+L), Mountain + Ocean (M+O), or Lakes + Ocean (L+O), giving golfers three different 18-hole experiences from a single property.

Mountain Course

  • Routes through elevated terrain with significant elevation changes
  • Panoramic views from the higher holes
  • Challenging elevation changes affect shot distance and club selection
  • The most dramatic of the three loops visually

Ocean Course

  • Stunning seaside vistas with fairways overlooking the Gulf of Thailand
  • The most photographed of the three nines
  • Coastal wind variability can impact play conditions
  • Routes that appear on Pattaya golf marketing imagery

Lakes Course

  • Tranquil and strategic layout with numerous water hazards
  • Lush surroundings — heavy tree cover, garden-style aesthetics
  • More demanding strategically — water in play on most holes
  • Best layout for players who enjoy risk-reward decision-making

Recommended Combinations

  • Casual / first-time visitors: Mountain + Ocean (most visually rewarding)
  • Strategic players wanting challenge: Lakes + Mountain (water + elevation combined)
  • Photographers and view-seekers: Mountain + Ocean for panoramic vistas
  • Multi-day visitors: Play all three loops across two rounds (M+L day 1, then Ocean nine on a 9-hole second visit)

Course Style

Phoenix Gold is built on wooded, rolling terrain in the Huay Yai countryside south of Pattaya. The design is resort-style championship, meaning:

  • Wide fairways — forgiving off the tee, accessible to mid and higher handicaps
  • Gentle elevation changes — visually interesting without being punishing
  • Water hazards — strategic water features especially on the Lakes nine
  • Scenic views of the surrounding hills, coastline, Gulf of Thailand
  • Wooded surrounds — mature trees framing fairways, forming visual corridors

The course is easier than Laem Chabang or Burapha for high-handicap players while still presenting enjoyable challenge for low handicaps from the back tees.
