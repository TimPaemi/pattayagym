★ About this venue

Cartoon Network Amazone Waterpark / Columbia Pictures Aquaverse

Overview

Cartoon Network Amazone Waterpark — rebranded in 2022 as Columbia Pictures Aquaverse — is the world's first Cartoon Network themed waterpark and one of Southeast Asia's largest theme-aquatic parks, located at 888 Moo 8, Na Jomtien, Sattahip, approximately 15 kilometres south of central Pattaya. Originally opened in 2014 as a partnership between Amazon Falls Co., Ltd. and Cartoon Network Enterprises, the park combined 18 different waterslides across 3 themed zones with Cartoon Network character integration including Ben 10, Adventure Time's Finn and Jake, The Powerpuff Girls, and Johnny Bravo, plus artificial sea, river rafting, FlowRider surfing, live shows, and rainforest-themed landscaping.

In 2022, the licensing transitioned and the park rebranded to Columbia Pictures Aquaverse — now featuring Sony Pictures intellectual property (Ghostbusters, Bad Boys, Jumanji, Hotel Transylvania, etc.) instead of Cartoon Network characters. The physical infrastructure remained largely the same — the slide hardware (manufactured by Polin Waterparks), zones, and most attractions are unchanged. Only the theming, character imagery, and naming of attractions shifted.

For Pattaya families and tourists looking for a full-day water-park experience with serious slide hardware (some of Asia's tallest) — and especially for those visiting with children who recognize the cartoon / movie IPs — Cartoon Network Amazone / Columbia Pictures Aquaverse remains one of the Eastern Seaboard's marquee family-tourism destinations.

The 2014 → 2022 IP Transition

Original Cartoon Network Era (2014-2021)

The park was launched in 2014 as the world's first Cartoon Network themed waterpark:

  • Cartoon Network Enterprises licensing for character integration
  • Ben 10 prominent — including the namesake Omnitrix sphere attraction
  • Adventure Time characters (Finn and Jake) integrated
  • Powerpuff Girls representations
  • Johnny Bravo character presence
  • Marketing pitch: kids who watched Cartoon Network would meet their TV heroes

2022 Columbia Pictures Aquaverse Rebrand

In 2022, the licensing partnership transitioned:

  • Columbia Pictures (Sony) took over as IP partner
  • Sony Pictures animated and live-action films became the new theme
  • Ghostbusters, Bad Boys, Jumanji, Hotel Transylvania integrated
  • Same physical infrastructure retained — slides, zones, layout largely unchanged
  • Theming refresh with Sony characters and branding
  • Park renamed Columbia Pictures Aquaverse — Cartoon Network Amazone branding retired

What Visitors Encounter (2026)

In 2026, visitors find the Columbia Pictures Aquaverse with Sony Pictures theming throughout. References to "Cartoon Network Amazone" persist online and in older travel content, but the on-the-ground experience is now Sony Pictures-themed.

For people remembering the original Cartoon Network Amazone, the slides themselves are mostly the same — just with new naming and themed visual treatment.

The 18-Slide / 3-Zone Layout

Omniverse Zone

The Cartoon Network era's signature zone with Ben 10 themes:

  • The Omnitrix — 23-metre tall sphere with digital projection and surround sound; 4-rider raft attraction inspired by Ben 10's alien device
  • Goop Loop — 12-metre freefall capsule with trap door release, then 360-degree loop
  • XLR8-TOR — 18-metre drop slide named after Ben 10's lightning-fast alien; one of the tallest and steepest rides in Asia
(In Columbia Pictures Aquaverse, these may be re-themed but physical hardware persists)

Adventure Zone

  • Multiple raft and body slides
  • Adventure Time-themed (originally) attractions
  • Family-friendly progressive slide difficulty

Cartoonival Zone

  • Lower-intensity slides for younger children
  • Splash pads and water playgrounds
  • Beginner / kids' slide network

Other Attractions

Beyond the 18 slides:

Artificial Sea / Wave Pool

  • Large wave pool with periodic wave generation
  • Beach-style entry
  • Lounger seating
  • Family-friendly

River Rafting

  • Lazy river circuit through park
  • Tube-based floating
  • Relaxation alternative to high-intensity slides

FlowRider

  • Stationary surfing wave for body and stand-up surfing
  • Rare attraction in Thailand
  • Coaching available for first-timers
  • Suitable for all skill levels

Live Shows

  • Character meet-and-greets during scheduled times
  • Stunt and dance shows
  • Themed entertainment

Polin Waterparks — Slide Manufacturer

All slides are supplied by Polin Waterparks — a globally significant water-attraction manufacturer:

  • Turkish company founded 1976
  • Premium waterpark slide manufacturer
  • Engineering-grade safety standards
  • Used in major waterparks globally
  • Polin's Asian footprint includes Cartoon Network Amazone and other regional projects
The Polin manufacturing pedigree means the slide hardware is engineering-quality rather than budget-tier — visitors experience proper international waterpark slide engineering.

Park Logistics

Hours

  • Daily 10:00-18:00 typical (verify current hours at park website)
  • Last entry typically 17:00
  • 8-hour park day allows multiple slide rotations

Pricing

Day pass pricing varies seasonally:

  • Adult day pass: ~฿1,200-1,800 typical
  • Child day pass: ~฿800-1,200
  • Discount tickets through Klook, Trazy, KKday, GetYourGuide
  • Annual pass options

Getting There

  • 15 km south of central Pattaya
  • 20-30 minute drive
  • Songthaew + walk possible from main road (Sukhumvit Highway)
  • Park shuttle services from many Pattaya hotels
  • Combined ticket+transport packages common via tour operators

Facilities

  • Locker rentals
  • Towel rentals
  • Food courts with multiple cuisines
  • Souvenir shops
  • First aid + lifeguards throughout
  • Wheelchair access at most attractions
  • Cabana rentals premium tier

Pros

  • World-first themed waterpark — historic significance
  • 18 slides across 3 zones — extensive variety
  • Polin Waterparks premium slide hardware
  • Among Asia's tallest/steepest slides (XLR8-TOR 18m, Omnitrix 23m)
  • FlowRider stationary surfing — rare in Thailand
  • Wave pool, lazy river, splash pads all included
  • Live shows + character meet-and-greets
  • Family-friendly all ages
  • Sony Pictures IP theming (post-2022) — Ghostbusters, Bad Boys, Jumanji
  • 15 km from central Pattaya — manageable day trip
  • Multiple language services for international tourists

Cons

  • Premium pricing — day pass not budget
  • Crowded peak season (high tourist months)
  • Long lines for most-popular slides
  • Outdoor + tropical heat — sunscreen and hydration critical
  • Distance from Pattaya — shuttle or transport required
  • IP rebrand confusion (Cartoon Network → Sony Pictures)
  • Limited adult-only zones — family park atmosphere throughout

Best For

  • Families with children ages 5-15 — primary demographic
  • Tourist day-trippers — full-day experience
  • Slide enthusiasts — 18 variety
  • Cartoon Network / Sony Pictures fans — IP recognition
  • Multi-generational groups — slides + lazy river + lounging
  • Hot-season visitors — water cools the heat
  • Birthday parties / group outings

Not Best For

  • Pure adult thrill-seekers — designed family-friendly
  • Quiet contemplation seekers — high energy and crowds
  • Pure budget tourists — day pass meaningful spend
  • Single-day Pattaya visitors with packed itineraries
  • Walking Street based travellers without transport
  • Rain-season visitors — wet days less appealing
