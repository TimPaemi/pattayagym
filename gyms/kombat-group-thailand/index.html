Overview

Kombat Group Thailand is a multi-discipline, all-inclusive, residential combat sports and fitness retreat set on a peaceful rural campus in Huai Yai, just east of Pattaya. It is consistently ranked 5/5 on TripAdvisor with 409 of 416 reviewers giving it the maximum score — making it one of the most universally praised combat sports facilities in all of Thailand. It was founded in 2004 by the late Christian Daghio, an Italian fight legend with 190 Muay Thai fights, 7 World Muay Thai titles, 2 WBA Asian Boxing titles, and the distinction of being the first Italian ever to fight and win at Lumpinee Stadium. After Daghio's tragic death in 2018, his vision and operational ethos have continued under his trained team — and the camp is widely described in 2025-2026 reviews as honoring his original standards meticulously.

The facility is structured around a residential, immersive model: you book a package (1 week, 2 weeks, 1 month, longer), you live on the campus, three meals a day are prepared in the on-site kitchen, and your training, food, sleep, recovery, and community are all integrated into one experience. It is the antithesis of a drop-in tourist gym and the best argument in Pattaya for the all-inclusive training holiday format.

For travellers wanting structure without the chaos of central Pattaya, multiple disciplines without resort-camp pricing, and a community that actively makes you better, Kombat Group is the closest thing Pattaya has to a guaranteed-quality experience.

The Founder — Christian Daghio (1969 – 2018)

To understand Kombat Group, you have to understand Christian Daghio. The camp is essentially the physical manifestation of one Italian fighter's quarter-century obsession with Thai combat sports.

Coming to Thailand

Daghio arrived in Thailand at age 21, in the early 1990s, with the dream of becoming a professional Muay Thai fighter. The early years were brutal. His training conditions were primitive — for a stretch he was literally training in an outdoor chicken coop, sharing space with fighters who lived rougher than most Western athletes can imagine. He embraced it. Over the next 25+ years he built a fighting career that eventually made him one of the most accomplished European fighters in the history of Thai combat sports.

Career Statistics

  • Muay Thai: 190 fights, 145 wins — including 7 World Muay Thai Championships
  • World titles in: WAKO, WMA, WMF, WTKA, WKL (Muay Thai sanctioning bodies)
  • First Italian to ever fight at Lumpinee Stadium — and to win there
  • Western Boxing: 35 matches (11 listed on BoxRec)
  • 2× WBA Asian Boxing Champion
  • 2× ABF (Asian Boxing Federation) Champion
  • 1× WBF International Boxing Champion
  • 5 MMA fights

He was, by the time he founded Kombat Group, one of the most credentialed European combat sports athletes alive.

Founding Kombat Group (2004)

Daghio founded Kombat Group in 2004 with a specific mission: to give "farang" (foreigners) the opportunity to try and master Muay Thai inside an authentic but supportive environment — bridging the gap between the no-frills, language-barrier-heavy traditional Thai camps and the resort-tourism Muay Thai vacations that were beginning to dominate the foreign market. The Huai Yai location was deliberate — far enough from central Pattaya to avoid distraction, close enough to remain accessible, set in lush gardens near Chak Nok Lake to provide the right atmosphere.

The Tragedy — October 26, 2018

On October 26, 2018, Daghio stepped into the ring for what would be his last fight at Rangsit Stadium in Bangkok, facing the Thai fighter Don Parueang for the vacant WBC Asia Silver Light Heavyweight Title.

Twenty seconds before the final bell of round 12, after being knocked down and rising, Daghio collapsed in the ring — struck by a sudden cerebral haemorrhage, attributed by physicians to the cumulative impact of his nearly 200 professional fights across decades. He was rushed to hospital and emergency surgery was performed in record time. He remained in a coma. Christian Daghio passed away on 2 November 2018 at age 49.

Continuing Legacy

Daghio's sudden death was a profound loss for the global Muay Thai community and especially for the Italian / European combat sports world. Kombat Group did not close. Under his trained operational team — including longtime manager Barbara and head instructor Cristiano (Black Belt BJJ + Krav Maga + MMA) — the camp has continued operating without interruption. Reviews from 2024-2026 repeatedly affirm that the standards, the philosophy, and the family-camp atmosphere remain unchanged. Daghio's fight memorabilia, photos, story, and ethos are deliberately woven into the camp's atmosphere. You train in his shadow, but the camp does not feel like a memorial — it feels like an active, evolving organism.

Disciplines & Programs

Kombat Group is genuinely multi-disciplinary — broader than almost any other Pattaya camp:

  • Muay Thai — flagship discipline, twice daily
  • Western Boxing — proper boxing program with dedicated rings; rare in Pattaya outside this camp
  • Mixed Martial Arts (MMA) — full curriculum, cage and mats
  • Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ) — black belt instruction, Gi and No-Gi
  • Krav Maga — practical self-defence, Israeli combat system
  • Weight Loss Bootcamp — flagship non-fighter program; combines martial arts + functional fitness + nutrition
  • Total Fitness Bootcamp — non-combat fitness program
  • Self-defence — separate from Krav Maga, more general
  • Aqua Fitness — pool-based conditioning
  • Beach Training — outdoor sessions, occasional
  • Random outdoor activities — built into longer-stay schedules

You can mix and match across disciplines. Many guests run a Muay Thai-as-flagship + BJJ-as-secondary combination. Krav Maga and MMA students often add boxing fundamentals.

Highly structured, designed to keep guests engaged from morning to evening:

Standard Day Structure

  • 08:00 — Day begins
  • 08:30-09:00 — Individual Running & Warm-Up (optional)
  • 09:00-10:30 — Muay Thai main class (or 09:00-10:00 Western Boxing)
  • 10:30-12:00 — Breakfast / recovery / pool time
  • 12:00-13:30 — Lunch (included in all-inclusive packages)
  • 13:30-16:30 — Rest / massage / activities / pool / off-campus excursions
  • 16:30-17:00 — Individual Running & Warm-Up (optional second slot)
  • 17:00-19:00 — Muay Thai evening session (Mon-Fri)
  • 19:00 — Dinner

BJJ Schedule (Specific)

  • Mon, Wed, Fri: 19:00-20:30
  • Tue, Thu: 07:30-09:00 (morning option)

Krav Maga / MMA / Self-defence

  • Slotted within the morning/evening windows; specific timing varies by week
  • Add-on to your daily Muay Thai routine

Sundays

  • No regular classes
  • Optional outdoor activities (beach training, pool sessions, off-camp excursions)
  • True rest day for body and mind

Special Activities

The camp deliberately injects random activities into the week to break monotony — Aqua Fitness, outdoor sessions, beach training, group excursions. The intention is to keep guests engaged across the full residential stay rather than letting fatigue/boredom set in.

Trainers

Specific named trainers identified in 2025-2026 sources:

  • Cristiano — Black Belt BJJ instructor, Krav Maga & MMA coach, Head Instructor for instructor courses
  • Joel — Western Boxing trainer, active pro boxer with WBC featherweight belt
  • Multiple Muay Thai Krus — including:
  • Intermediate-level Kru focusing on Muay Thai + Fitness blend
  • Advanced Kru with personal fight record (24 fights: 8W-11L-5D)
  • Barbara — Manager (operational)

The trainer roster has been described in dozens of reviews as "competent, friendly, follows your progress personally" — students never feel lost or unguided. Communication languages: English, Italian, Spanish, Thai — reflecting the international (and especially European) clientele.

Facility — In Detail

This is what a serious, intentional all-inclusive combat sports campus looks like:

Combat Sports Spaces

  • 800 m² dedicated Muay Thai training area with:
  • 4 full-size Muay Thai rings
  • Multiple heavy bags, banana bags, uppercut bags, wall bags
  • Speed balls, double-end balls
  • Pad-holding stations
  • Mat zones for clinching and floor work
  • 500 m² dedicated Boxing gym with:
  • 2 boxing rings
  • Full Western boxing bag line-up (heavy, speed, double-end, body)
  • Conditioning equipment specific to boxing footwork
  • Dedicated MMA cage for cage work
  • BJJ mat area with full grappling space
  • All spaces cleaned and disinfected twice daily

Strength, Conditioning & Recovery

  • Free weights, kettlebells, TRX, jump ropes, medicine balls
  • Cardio machines (rowers, bikes, treadmills)
  • Massage room — daily massage available (included in some packages)
  • Outdoor swimming pool — for laps, recovery, and Aqua Fitness classes

Lifestyle Amenities

  • On-site restaurant / canteen — Thai chef preparing 3 meals daily for athletes; healthy, balanced, performance-focused
  • Coffee shop
  • Garden / pool deck communal area — central social space
  • Daily cleaning service — rooms turned over fast, laundry sometimes done in hours

The entire campus is set in a lush tropical garden near Chak Nok Lake — described in dozens of reviews as a "peaceful oasis," "a serene rural enclave," and the antidote to central Pattaya's chaos.

Accommodation

Multiple room tiers integrated with training packages:

  • Deluxe Room (Single/Double): Spacious, nestled around the swimming pool, with mini-fridge, kettle (complimentary tea/coffee), smart TV
  • Standard Room (Single/Double): Comfortable AC rooms, well-furnished
  • Shared Accommodation: Budget option for solo travellers willing to share

All rooms get daily cleaning and the laundry service is repeatedly praised as fast and reliable.

Pricing

Three primary tiers: budget, standard, all-inclusive luxury. All prices in Thai Baht.

Drop-In / Non-Residential Rates

  • Drop-in single session (no accommodation): ~฿700 (~$21)
  • Monthly Muay Thai membership (no accommodation): ~฿9,000 (~$274)

All-Inclusive Residential Packages (Indicative)

  • 1 week all-inclusive: ~฿15,000-25,000 depending on tier
  • 2 weeks all-inclusive: ~฿25,000-40,000
  • 1 month all-inclusive: ~฿45,000-70,000
  • Specific current rates: kombatgroup.com/prices/

What's Included in All-Inclusive

  • Accommodation (your chosen tier)
  • Three meals daily (Thai chef, performance-focused)
  • Daily supplements: 2 protein shakes with creatine, water/lemon electrolyte drink, magnesium glycinate, multivitamin
  • All training across disciplines (Muay Thai, Boxing, BJJ, Krav Maga, MMA, Weight Loss, etc.)
  • Pool access, garden, lounge
  • Daily cleaning
  • Access to organised weekly outdoor activities (beach training, excursions)

What's NOT Included

  • VAT 7%
  • Credit card processing fee 3%
  • Airfare and airport taxi (separately bookable)
  • Off-campus food/drinks
  • Massage upgrades beyond included sessions
  • Premium spa services
  • Off-camp excursion costs

Booking Mechanics

  • ฿5,000 deposit required to guarantee your room
  • Outstanding amount paid on arrival
  • Within 24-48 hours of inquiry, the camp sends a clear reply with pricing, availability, and program plan tailored to your goal

Reputation Summary

Kombat Group is the safest, most predictably high-quality combat sports residential experience in the Pattaya region. It is particularly strong with:

  • European travellers (especially Italian, Spanish-speaking — Daghio's legacy + Cristiano's languages)
  • Beginners on transformation programs (Weight Loss success stories are real)
  • Travellers who want zero-logistics holidays (everything is handled — book and arrive)
  • Couples/groups doing fitness retreats together
  • Multi-discipline cross-trainers wanting BJJ + Muay Thai + Boxing under one roof
  • Returning students (return-customer rate is exceptionally high)

Where Fairtex offers institutional polish at the cost of attentive consistency, and Sityodtong offers lineage at the cost of facility comfort, Kombat Group offers a curated, predictable, structured experience that may be the single most consistent product in the Pattaya combat sports market.

Common questions about Kombat Group Thailand

Where is Kombat Group Thailand?

Kombat Group Thailand is located in Huai Yai / East Pattaya (rural, near Chak Nok Lake), Pattaya, Thailand. Full address: 49/1 Moo 4, Soi Chaknok 19, Huai Yai, Bang Lamung District, Chonburi 20150, Thailand.

What are the opening hours of Kombat Group Thailand?

Kombat Group Thailand operates Day starts 08:00, ends ~19:00; classes Mon-Sat (Sundays no regular classes). Hours can change on Thai public holidays — call ahead or check their website to confirm.

How much does Kombat Group Thailand cost?

Kombat Group Thailand is in the ฿฿฿ muay thai price tier — premium (฿1,000-2,000 drop-in, ฿15,000-30,000/month with English-speaking trainers). Exact current rates change with season and group size; contact the venue to confirm.

Is Kombat Group Thailand good for beginners?

Most Pattaya Muay Thai camps welcome beginners. Contact Kombat Group Thailand directly to ask about their beginner programs and what to expect on day one.

Do staff at Kombat Group Thailand speak English?

Pattaya is an international tourist city, and most venues have at least one English-speaking staff member. Contact Kombat Group Thailand ahead of your visit if you need language confirmation.

Does Kombat Group Thailand offer on-site accommodation?

Yes — Kombat Group Thailand provides on-site or partner accommodation. This makes it a strong fit for full-immersion training camps and longer-stay visitors. Contact the venue for room types, pricing and minimum stay.

How do I book or contact Kombat Group Thailand?

Kombat Group Thailand is reachable via phone (+66 81 099 9333), their website, Facebook DM. Most Pattaya venues respond fastest to Facebook or Instagram messages. For same-day visits, call ahead to confirm session times.
