Diving Pattaya — how operators differ

Pattaya is a training and fun-dive hub, not Similan liveaboard territory — but Koh Larn, wreck sites, and day boats run year-round. Operators differ on boat size, instructor ratio, equipment age, and English depth.

Where boats leave from

Central Bali Hai Pier and south-side marinas — many divers stay Jomtien or Na Jomtien and taxi to pier. Combine dive days with hotel pool recovery on luxury clubs list.

Training + fitness same trip

Open-water courses are 3–4 day commitments — plan gym around confined-water sessions. Cross-training: best for beginners (other sports) · compare dive shops.

Koh Larn day trip venue: Koh Larn Coral Island · Watersports hub: all watersports.

Choosing PADI vs SSI shop

Both certify Open Water globally. PADI dominates marketing; SSI shops like Dive Station suit flexible online theory. IDC-heavy shops (Aquanauts, Mermaid's) matter if you might become an instructor — not for a one-week holiday try-dive.

Ask: boat size, diver-to-guide ratio, equipment age, refund policy on weather cancellation. Wreck dives need advanced cert — do not book HTMS Khram penetration on holiday Open Water. Broader watersport map: diving & watersports guide.

Common questions

How much does Open Water dive certification cost in Pattaya?

PADI Open Water typically ฿11,000–฿16,000 over 3–4 days. SSI similar. Includes manual, gear, boat trips, and pool/confined-water sessions.

Where do Pattaya dive boats go?

Coral Island (Koh Larn), Koh Sak, Koh Krok, Koh Khrok for shallow reefs. HTMS Khram (artificial reef wreck, ~30m) for advanced divers. Most operators run half-day or full-day boats with 2–3 dives.

Are Pattaya dive sites good for beginners?

Yes — most reef dives are 8–18m with calm, clear water year-round. Visibility peaks Nov–Apr (15–25m). Gulf of Thailand currents are mild compared to Andaman Sea destinations.
