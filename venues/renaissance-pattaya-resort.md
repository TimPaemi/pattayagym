Renaissance Pattaya Resort & Spa is the **Marriott Bonvoy "Renaissance"-branded sister property** to the larger Pattaya Marriott Resort and Spa, both sitting on the **Na Jomtien beachfront** strip. Opened in **2017** as a purpose-built 257-room property across 10 floors, Renaissance complements the broader Marriott Bonvoy presence in Pattaya by offering a more **boutique-modern** aesthetic — Renaissance is Marriott's "lifestyle" brand emphasising design, local culture integration, and slightly hipper guest experience than mainline Marriott Hotels.

For travellers earning Bonvoy points and choosing among Marriott Pattaya properties, the choice is largely a matter of style:

- **Pattaya Marriott Resort and Spa** — classic full-service resort experience, family programme depth
- **Renaissance Pattaya Resort & Spa** — design-led modern architecture, slightly younger demographic, similar 5-star amenities

Both are 15–20 minutes south of central Pattaya by Bolt, both fronting the long Na Jomtien sandy beach, both within Marriott Bonvoy with full points + status integration.

## What's there

**Rooms:** 257 across 10 floors — most with Gulf of Thailand sea views or pool views

**Pools:**
- Two outdoor swimming pools (typical configuration: main pool + adults pool)
- Kids' pool — separate, shallow, supervised
- Sun deck areas with loungers and pool butler service

**Fitness Centre:**
- Modern equipment (Life Fitness or Technogym typical for Marriott group)
- Extended daily hours
- Personal training bookable
- Yoga / stretching area

**Full-Service Spa:**
- Single-treatment rooms + couples treatment rooms
- Traditional Thai massage, aromatherapy, body scrubs
- Half-day and full-day spa packages
- Hydrotherapy / sauna / steam access

**Kids' Club:**
- Supervised children's activities
- Age-appropriate programming
- Family rooms / connecting rooms available

**Other amenities:**
- Multiple dining outlets (international, Thai, beachside grill typical)
- Beach access — direct onto Na Jomtien sand
- Meeting / event spaces
- Free Wi-Fi
- Spa treatment booking 24-48 hr ahead recommended

## Distinctive vs. Pattaya Marriott Resort

**Renaissance distinctives:**
- **Younger build (2017)** — modern architecture, more contemporary aesthetic
- **Renaissance brand positioning** — design-forward, local-culture integration in art and dining
- **Smaller-feeling** than the larger sister property, more boutique vibe
- **Hipper restaurant programme** typically — Renaissance brand favours destination dining

**Pattaya Marriott Resort distinctives:**
- **Larger footprint** with more facilities and pool count
- **More established family programming**
- **Classic full-service resort feel**

For Bonvoy members, the choice often comes down to availability + points-redemption strategy. Both deliver the 5-star Marriott base; Renaissance leans modern-design, Marriott leans classic-resort.

## How to visit

For overnight stays, book through marriott.com / Bonvoy or major OTAs. For day-pass: call **+66 38 259 099** to confirm availability and rates. For spa-only bookings: book directly through the spa, ideally 24–48 hr ahead on weekends.

For travellers planning a Na Jomtien-focused luxury day, the canonical multi-stop is:

1. **Pattaya Marriott Resort** (this venue's sister) — morning pool + spa
2. **Renaissance Pattaya** — lunch at the Renaissance restaurant
3. **Andaz Pattaya Jomtien** (Hyatt) — afternoon onsen + spa
4. **Mövenpick Siam Hotel** — sunset
5. **Ocean Marina Jomtien** — dinner at the marina

Five 5-star properties + the largest marina in SE Asia, all within a 20-minute Bolt radius along Na Jomtien. It's the densest 5-star cluster in Thailand outside Bangkok and Phuket — and Renaissance is one of its newest entries.
