Jomtien Beach Volleyball Courts

Overview

Jomtien Beach Volleyball Courts are Pattaya's primary beach volleyball community — a network of sand courts along Jomtien Beach where daily pickup games unite local Thai players with international visitors and the long-stay expat community. Unlike commercial sports facilities with fixed schedules and fees, Jomtien beach volleyball is genuinely community-driven: courts get set up by regular players, anyone can join games, and the atmosphere is welcoming, friendly, and free.

Volleyball is one of the most popular participating sports for local Thai people — Thailand's national women's team has competed at Asian championships and Olympics, building a strong domestic base. Beach volleyball specifically has grown internationally as Olympic exposure (Atlanta 1996 onward) made the sand discipline globally mainstream. Pattaya's combination of long sandy beaches, year-round outdoor weather, and large international community makes it natural beach volleyball territory.

The @pattaya_beach_volley Instagram community organizes regular meetups, tournaments, and skills sessions. Multiple court locations exist along Jomtien Beach plus other Pattaya beaches, but Jomtien is the recognized center of activity.

Where to Play

Jomtien Beach (Primary)

The 6 km Jomtien Beach has multiple court locations:

  • Central Jomtien — most active, near hotel cluster
  • South Jomtien — quieter end, occasional tournaments
  • Near junction of Pratamnak and Jomtien districts — popular meeting point

Court setup is typically permanent or semi-permanent — nets installed by regulars or the @pattaya_beach_volley community, with sand kept clean by daily play.

Wong Amat Beach (Secondary)

A single court at Wong Amat Beach in north Pattaya/Naklua area — quieter community, sometimes preferable for those staying north.

North Pattaya

Various scattered courts.

Daily Schedule (Informal)

Typical Pattern

  • Morning (07:00-10:00): quieter, casual practice, lighter players
  • Late afternoon (15:00-18:00): primary pickup play window
  • Evening (18:00-21:00): social games, light fading
  • Saturday/Sunday: busiest days, tournaments common
  • Avoid midday (11:00-15:00) — sand too hot, sun punishing

How Pickup Works

  1. Show up at active court
  2. Watch a couple of games to gauge skill level
  3. Ask "next game?" to current players
  4. Wait your turn — players cycle through
  5. Pay nothing — pure community sport
  6. Bring water — hot sand demands hydration

Skill Levels

  • Casual / fun: anyone can play, no prior experience needed
  • Intermediate: some skill expected, played 2v2 or 4v4
  • Competitive: skilled players, 2v2 sand format, decent serves and spikes
  • Tournaments: organised via Instagram and Facebook

Beach Volleyball Specifics

How Beach Differs from Indoor

  • Sand surface — softer, slower, demands more leg power
  • 2v2 format typical (vs. indoor 6v6)
  • Smaller court (16m × 8m vs. 18m × 9m indoor)
  • Lighter ball with lower pressure
  • More hand and finger discipline required (sand makes hands sticky)
  • Outdoor wind / sun affects play
  • No formal substitutions in 2v2

Why Beach Volleyball Is Popular Globally

  • Olympic sport since 1996
  • Lifestyle appeal — beach + sport combination
  • Doubles social — 2v2 format is intense and conversational
  • Year-round in tropical destinations
  • No specialised equipment — just sand and net

Pricing

Beach volleyball is genuinely free:

  • Court use: free (community-maintained)
  • Equipment: bring your own ball / borrow from regulars
  • Net + lines: community-installed
  • Tournaments: modest entry fees ($5-20) for formal events

This is the cheapest organised sport in Pattaya alongside running with the Hash and pétanque.

Equipment Needed

Minimum Kit

  • Volleyball (regulation indoor or beach — beach is lighter and pressurized lower)
  • Sunscreen
  • Water / electrolytes
  • Beach towel (sit/recover between games)
  • Sun hat / sunglasses for non-active periods
  • Closed-toe sand shoes optional (most play barefoot)

Better Kit

  • Beach volleyball ball (Mikasa, Wilson, Spalding)
  • Sand socks (protect feet from hot sand)
  • Sport sunscreen (sweat-resistant)
  • Visor (replaces hat for play)
  • Spike shorts for women / players

Social Scene

Demographics

  • Mixed Thai and international — Russian, German, British, French, Brazilian common
  • Both genders play actively
  • Mostly 20s-50s demographic
  • Skill range — total beginners to former pro players

How to Get In

  • Facebook: Beach Volleyball Thailand group (regional but lots of Pattaya content)
  • Show up at courts in late afternoon
  • Follow active players — community tags spots and sessions
