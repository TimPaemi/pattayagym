Diving & watersports.

By TimPaemi · 6 min read · Updated 2026-06-02 · How we rank →

Pattaya splits into scuba boat ops, kite and wing at Jomtien, marina sailing south, and beach toys on Koh Larn — twenty-one watersports venues in our directory. This guide maps each lane without inventing prices.

Pattaya watersport splits into four lanes — scuba diving (Bali Hai pier boat ops), kitesurf and wing (Jomtien and Blue Lagoon), sailing and yachting (Ocean Marina south), and beach toys (parasail, jet ski, Koh Larn day trips). Scuba diving — Bali Hai pier belt

Most Pattaya dive boats leave Bali Hai Pier on morning schedules — fun dives to nearby islands, wreck sites like HTMS Khram, and Open Water courses over 3–4 days. Shops cluster central Pattaya and Jomtien; boats are shared infrastructure.

  • Adventure Divers — since 1998, own boat MV Saifon, wreck specialist, English/Russian/Thai.
  • Aquanauts — British/Dutch PADI 5-Star CDC + National Geographic centre, tech pipeline.
  • Real Divers — Chandler brothers since 2005, family-run PADI 5-Star IDC, 12m speedboat.
  • Mermaid's Dive Center — PADI 5-Star IDC, Jomtien + Beach Road + Bangkok branch.
  • Jomtien Dive Center — beach-side IDC on Jomtien, full course progression.
  • Dive Station — SSI operation, snorkel + first-timer friendly, European visitor base.

Kitesurf, wingfoil, and cable wake

  • KBA Kiteboarding Asia — Blue Lagoon Na Jomtien, Nov–Mar wind season peak, Thailand multi-location chain.
  • Kitesurf Pattaya — IKO-certified south Jomtien, 3-day beginner package ~$250.
  • Clubloongchat — Na Jomtien beach school: kitesurf, wingfoil, windsurf, sailing, SUP.
  • Thai Wake Park — Pattaya's only cable wake park east near Mabprachan, beginner + advanced lakes.

Wind-dependent sports need morning checks — call same-day if gusts drop. Pair with east-side gym days: East Pattaya guide.

Sailing, marina, and yacht club

Ocean Marina Jomtien is Southeast Asia's largest marina — 455 berths, yachts to 75m, Top of the Gulf Regatta host, customs clearance for international arrivals.

  • SailBreeze Sailing School — 20+ years at Ocean Marina, IYT certification + yacht charters.
  • Royal Varuna Yacht Club — Pratamnak Hill, 60+ year prestige club, RYA training, ILCA/Optimist regattas Jan–Mar.

Beach days and island trips

  • Koh Larn (Coral Island) — 30-min ferry from Bali Hai, six beaches, parasail, jet ski, intro scuba, clearer water than mainland.
  • Wave Pattaya — multi-sport beach operator Pattaya + Jomtien (parasail, banana boat, SUP, kayak).
  • Underwater World — aquarium with shark dive for certified divers; family rainy-day option.

Quick pick by traveller type

  • First Open Water course? → Real Divers or Aquanauts (IDC track) · ranked list for tiers.
  • Certified fun diver on holiday? → Adventure Divers wreck days or Mermaid's day trips.
  • Kite beginner Nov–Mar? → KBA Blue Lagoon or Kitesurf Pattaya south Jomtien.
  • Training holiday rest day? → Koh Larn ferry + beach — no heavy gear.
  • Luxury sailing week? → SailBreeze at Ocean Marina or Royal Varuna membership path.
  • Family intro to ocean? → Underwater World + Wave beach toys — not dive course pressure.

Pairing dive days with land training

Typical rhythm: morning boat dive 09:00–14:00 · afternoon Muay Thai pads or gym · evening recovery swim. Base south for marina/kite: Jomtien camps (Jomtien gyms). Base central for Bali Hai pier shops: Central Pattaya.

Gulf of Thailand diving — season and visibility

Pattaya diving is Gulf water, not Andaman clarity. Visibility on fun dives often runs 5–12 metres depending on tide, rain runoff, and boat traffic near islands. The trade-off is convenience: you can certify, fun-dive wrecks, and be back at your hotel by lunch without domestic flights to the islands.

November–February is the most comfortable surface interval — lower heat, calmer mornings, better kite wind overlap on rest days. March–May is hot but workable with early boat departures. May–October rainy season means more cancelled boats; shops reschedule or refund per their policy — confirm in writing before paying large course deposits.

Certification paths (first timer to instructor track)

Try dive / Discover Scuba — one pool or shallow day, no certification. Good if you are unsure before a 3–4 day Open Water course. Open Water — entry-level licence; Pattaya shops run 3–4 day schedules with classroom, confined water, and open-water dives. Advanced / specialties — wreck, deep, nitrox where the shop has instructors on staff. Divemaster and IDC — Aquanauts and Mermaid's run instructor pipelines; longer stays pair with visa planning.

Ask whether your course uses PADI or SSI materials — both are widely accepted; cross-over rules differ if you trained elsewhere years ago.

Rent vs bring your own kit

Reputable shops include BCD, reg, weights, and tanks in course and fun-dive pricing. Bringing your own mask and computer is worth the bag space. Wetsuits are optional in 28–30°C water; a rash guard suffices for many divers. Tech divers heading to HTMS Khram should confirm twin-set and stage availability with Adventure Divers or Aquanauts in advance.

Flying, ears, and insurance

Standard advice: no flight within 12–18 hours after a single no-deco fun dive; longer after repetitive deep days — your instructor will brief. Sinus and ear issues are common in humid Pattaya; see Pattaya Medical before forcing dives with congestion. Travel insurance that explicitly covers scuba is not optional for wreck and deep profiles.

Sample 5-day dive + train week

  1. Mon–Wed: Morning Open Water or fun dives from Bali Hai · afternoon optional gym day pass.
  2. Thu: Rest day — pool laps (swimming guide) or Koh Larn beach without tanks.
  3. Fri: Wreck or advanced dive · evening watch MAX Muay Thai.
  4. Weekend: Kite at Blue Lagoon if wind holds — Jomtien base keeps commutes short.

Boat day logistics from Bali Hai

Arrive pier 30–45 minutes before departure — paperwork and gear fitting eat time. Seasickness: sit aft, hydrate, avoid heavy alcohol night before. Camera policies vary — ask before bringing GoPro on wreck penetrations. Tip boat crew modestly if service is strong — not mandatory but appreciated on liveaboard-style days.

Nitrox and advanced divers

Tech-friendly shops (Aquanauts, Adventure Divers) handle mixed gas and wreck penetration when you show certs — book ahead, not walk-in. Fun divers should not piggyback tech profiles — depth limits exist for a reason. Post-dive beer is cultural but dehydrates — water first, Chang second.
