Holiday Inn Pattaya is the IHG mid-luxury Beach Road sister to the upscale InterContinental Pattaya Resort up on Pratamnak Hill. Where InterContinental commands the hilltop, Holiday Inn commands the beachfront — directly opposite Pattaya Beach with two-tower architecture giving most rooms a sea view and two infinity pools suspended high above the boardwalk.

For families, mid-budget travelers, and weekenders who want central beachfront position + reliable IHG-grade fitness/spa stack without the full ฿฿฿฿ premium of a 5-star hotel, Holiday Inn Pattaya is one of the strongest pure-value picks in the city.

Position in Pattaya market

The Beach Road hotel stack splits into:

  • Hilton Pattaya — 5-star ฿฿฿฿, mall-attached, 16th-floor infinity pool
  • InterContinental Pattaya Resort — 5-star ฿฿฿฿, Pratamnak hilltop, 3 pools + tennis
  • Holiday Inn Pattaya — 4-star ฿฿฿, Beach Road central, 2 infinity pools (this venue)
  • Centara Grand Mirage — 5-star ฿฿฿฿, Wong Amat, family/water park
  • Hard Rock Hotel — 4-star ฿฿฿, central, largest free-form pool in SE Asia

Holiday Inn's distinctives:

  • True Beach Road position — across the road from Pattaya Beach itself
  • Two outdoor infinity pools at different tower levels — cardio swim mornings, sunset cocktails afternoons
  • Family-friendly default — Kids Club + family pool baked into the operation
  • Bay-view fitness centre — large windows over Pattaya Bay (rare among 4-star hotel gyms)
  • IHG loyalty programme — One Rewards points + status benefits
  • More affordable than the 5-star tier — full sport-and-spa stack at ~30% lower entry rate

Fitness centre

The fitness centre features modern cardio equipment, free weights, and strength training machines, all positioned in front of large windows that open onto Pattaya Bay views. The light and the view alone make this one of the more pleasant hotel gyms in the city to actually train in — most hotel-gym corners are buried behind back-of-house.

The two infinity pools

Holiday Inn's signature visual feature is its two outdoor infinity pools:

1. Bay Tower — Level 4 infinity pool — quieter, fronting Pattaya Bay, classic infinity-edge design with the sea on the horizon. 2. Executive Tower — Level 6 infinity pool — slightly higher, similar bay-facing infinity edge.

Both pools are set against the Pattaya Bay backdrop with sun loungers, towel service, and cocktail service. Operating hours 06:00–20:00 daily — useful for early-morning swim laps before tourists fill the deck.

A separate family pool + Kids Club keeps families and young children appropriately catered for without competing for adult deck space at the infinity pools.

Tea Tree Spa

The on-site Tea Tree Spa offers:

  • Facial treatments
  • Body scrubs
  • Hot stone massage
  • Traditional Thai and aromatherapy oil massage
  • Multi-step packages

It's not as expansive as Hilton's eforea or InterContinental's Amburaya, but it's competently run and priced more accessibly.

Family-friendly programming

Holiday Inn Pattaya is explicitly family-positioned in Pattaya's hotel market — competing with Centara Grand Mirage and Hard Rock for the kids-and-parents demographic:

  • Kids Club with supervised activities
  • Family pool separate from the infinity adults pools
  • Family rooms with bunk-bed setups
  • Kid-friendly dining options
  • Beach access directly across the road

How to visit

For overnight stays, book through ihg.com or major OTAs. For day-pass: call +66 38 725 555 — Holiday Inn's day-pass programme is less established than the 5-star tier but spa-only bookings are typically welcomed. Free underground parking for self-drivers.

For travelers planning a Beach Road central day with multiple stops, pair Holiday Inn with Hilton Pattaya (3 min walk south for the 16th-floor infinity pool day-pass), Hard Rock pool (3 min walk for the largest free-form pool in SE Asia), and Pattaya Beach itself (across the road). Central Festival Mall is 5 min walk for shopping/F&B between sessions.
