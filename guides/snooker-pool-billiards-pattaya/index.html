Pool & snooker.

By Tim Paemi · 12 min read · Updated 2026-06-01 · How we rank →

Pattaya billiards ranges from bar coin-op tables to Megabreak's tournament 9-foot setup and SF Strike Bowl's mall bowling-plus-pool. This guide maps dedicated halls, pricing bands, and how cue sport fits a Muay Thai or gym week.

Pattaya · 158 venues hand-checked · Compare gyms

Pattaya cue sport splits into three tiers: bar pool (coin-op, warped cloth), sports bars with a few tables, and dedicated halls with tournament-grade 9-foot tables and proper lighting. This guide covers only the third tier plus the best hybrid venues in our 158-venue directory — where equipment gives honest feedback, not holiday flukes.

Pool and snooker are not cardio, but they fit a training week as low-impact evenings, rainy-season backup, and social sport for digital nomads and long-stay groups. Start at clubs & social sport, shortlist on compare, and map hotels on plan my trip.

Pool vs snooker vs bar tables — know what you are booking

  • American pool (8-ball / 9-ball) — most dedicated halls; Megabreak runs tournament-class 9-foot tables (regulation size). Bar tables are often 7–8 foot — different geometry.
  • Snooker — larger table, smaller balls; IQ-style halls and Megabreak often list snooker — confirm table availability on arrival.
  • Bowling + pool combo — SF Strike Bowl and Pattaya Bowl bundle lanes with billiards for groups who want variety in one visit.

1. Megabreak Pool Hall — serious 9-foot pool (Soi Diana)

Megabreak Pool Hall on Soi Diana (Soi 13) off Second Road is Pattaya's longest-running expat favourite for dedicated pool — described in venue research as tournament-class 9-foot tables in a modern, well-lit arena (not a dark bar corner). Clientele mixes visiting serious players, long-stay British/Aussie/German/Russian regulars, and local league talent.

Why 9-foot matters: smaller bar tables forgive bad position; 9-foot American setup is what most competitive players train on abroad. Megabreak is where you practice fundamentals — bridge, stroke, rack running — without fighting bad cloth.

Practical notes: hourly hire; cue rental and chalk usually on-site; snooker tables often available — verify when you walk in. Walking distance from central hotels; 10 minutes south of Walking Street. Tell drivers "Soi Diana, Megabreak."

2. SF Strike Bowl — mall bowling + pool (Central Festival)

SF Strike Bowl Pattaya sits on the 6th floor of Central Festival Pattaya Beach Mall on Beach Road — 16 regular bowling lanes + 2 VIP partitioned lanes, integrated pool tables, arcade, and a smoking lounge with sea view. Operated by SF Group (major Thailand cinema/entertainment chain). Hours on file: daily 12:00–22:00.

Pricing snapshot from venue file (confirm at desk): ~฿180 per person per game bowling; ฿50 socks if you do not bring your own. Pool pricing is separate from bowling — ask at counter. Languages listed: English, Thai, Russian, Chinese.

Best for: families, birthday groups, post-cinema entertainment, tourists who want air-con and mall parking. Not a league-serious pool hall — pair Megabreak for technique nights and SF Strike for social Fridays.

3. Pattaya Bowl — late-night North Pattaya (Soi Regent Marina)

Pattaya Bowl at 463/78 Moo 9, Soi Regent Marina, Pattaya 2nd Road runs daily 10:00–02:00 — one of the city's classic late-night bowling alleys with modern lanes, hourly hire, arcade, and food. ฿฿ tier. Good when Megabreak closes and you still want indoor group sport without club noise.

Compare to SF Strike: Pattaya Bowl wins on late hours; SF Strike wins on mall convenience + VIP lanes + Beach Road walkability.

4. Club lounges and hotel tables — member rules

Pattaya Sports Club and several hotel bars maintain pool tables for members or guests — policies change; confirm table fees and guest access on arrival. For racquet-first social clubs see tennis & badminton guide and running & clubs guide.

Central Pattaya pool hall map (beyond our directory)

Long-stay players also cite Double Kiss (Third Road), IQ Snooker & Pool (3rd Road junction), and Legends Pool & Sports Bar (Pattaya Klang) — we list Megabreak and SF Strike as verified directory venues; explore others on foot if you base on Second Road.

Pricing and equipment — what to expect

  • Hourly table hire — typically ฿100–฿300/hour for standard pool; snooker may cost more.
  • Peak nights — Friday–Saturday at SF Strike and Megabreak can queue in high season; arrive before 20:00 or book groups ahead.
  • House cues included at most halls; serious players travel with their own tip, chalk, and glove.
  • League play — Megabreak has historically hosted league nights; ask staff for current schedule.

FAQ

Where is the best serious pool in Pattaya?

Megabreak Pool Hall on Soi Diana is the most consistently cited dedicated 9-foot tournament setup in our files. SF Strike Bowl is better for groups wanting bowling, arcade, and mall convenience.

Does SF Strike Bowl have snooker tables?

SF Strike emphasises bowling and general billiards; for snooker-specific tables, confirm at Megabreak or dedicated snooker halls on 3rd Road. Always ask staff on arrival — table mix changes.

How much does an evening of pool cost?

Budget ฿200–฿600 per person for 2–3 hours at dedicated halls depending on table count and drinks. Bowling at SF Strike adds ~฿180/game plus socks. Confirm current rates at the counter — we do not publish live prices.

Can I walk to Megabreak from Beach Road hotels?

Yes — Soi Diana is central; 10–15 minutes on foot from many Beach Road bases, or a short baht-bus. From Jomtien, plan a taxi — see Jomtien guide if you train south and play pool central.

Is pool a good rest day for Muay Thai training?

Yes — low impact, no leg pounding. Avoid marathon bar sessions before morning pads; alcohol and late nights matter more than pool itself. Fight campers: training holiday guide.

What should I verify before a group booking?

Number of tables available, peak-hour queue policy, minimum spend, and whether snooker vs pool tables are reserved. For bowling + pool combos, confirm SF Strike or Pattaya Bowl lane booking for 6+ people.

Safety and venue etiquette

Dedicated halls are quieter than Walking Street bars — still treat bags and phones as theft targets. Chalk dust and cigarette smoke vary by venue; Megabreak leans serious-player atmosphere while SF Strike is family-mall. Tip staff fairly on long sessions; league players often reserve tables by name on peak nights. If you travel with your own cue, check case storage at the counter.
