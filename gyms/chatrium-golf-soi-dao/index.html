★ About this venue

Chatrium Golf Resort Soi Dao Chanthaburi

Overview

Chatrium Golf Resort Soi Dao is the most remote, mountain-set, and visually distinctive golf destination in the Pattaya extended golf circuit — a Dennis Griffiths-designed 18-hole championship course in the Khao Soi Dao Mountains approximately 3 hours drive from Pattaya in Chanthaburi province. Where most Pattaya-area courses sit in coastal lowlands within 30-50 minutes of the city, Chatrium Golf is genuinely a destination golf experience — far enough that golfers stay overnight at the on-site 4-star Chatrium-managed 47-room hotel rather than commute.

The course was originally designed by Dennis Griffiths — the same architect responsible for Phoenix Gold Golf and Country Club in the Pattaya area — and redesigned in 2015 by local Thai architect Pirapon Namatra of Golf East with hole rerouting and refinements. The result is a 7,144-yard par-72 course that runs through 395 acres of mountain terrain with emerald-green landscapes, water hazards, and the signature par-3 14th as its defining hole.

For golfers who want a genuinely distant, mountain-set, multi-day golf experience that combines golf with hospitality and nature — and for Pattaya-based golf travellers who want a 3-day weekend escape from the coast — Chatrium Golf Resort Soi Dao is the most distinct experience available within the broader Pattaya golf circuit.

The Soi Dao Mountains Setting

Geographic Context

Khao Soi Dao Mountains (เขาสอยดาว) is a mountain range in Chanthaburi province, eastern Thailand:

  • Mountain range running through Chanthaburi
  • Forested upland terrain with elevation gain
  • Cooler temperatures than coastal Pattaya — meaningful relief in hot months
  • Rural setting — limited tourist density
  • Adjacent to Khao Soi Dao Wildlife Sanctuary — protected area with wildlife
  • Chanthaburi province is famous for fruit (durians, rambutans, mangosteens) and gem mining (rubies, sapphires)

Why It Matters for Golf

The mountain setting delivers:

  • Cooler playing conditions — significant in March-May hot season (often 5-8°C cooler than Pattaya)
  • Visual drama with mountain backdrop on every hole
  • Less commercial development intrusion
  • Wildlife and bird presence — natural environment integration
  • Elevation variation in course routing
  • Genuine destination feel — not a commuter golf course

Course Designer — Dennis Griffiths

Original Design

The course was designed by Dennis Griffiths, an American architect with significant Asia portfolio:

  • Phoenix Gold Golf and Country Club in Pattaya — same designer, allowing comparison
  • Multiple courses across Asia
  • Mid-tier American school approach — strategic but not extreme

2015 Redesign by Pirapon Namatra

In 2015, Thai architect Pirapon Namatra of Golf East undertook a significant redesign:

  • Holes were redesigned for improved playability
  • Routing was altered to refine the experience
  • Modernization of agronomy and infrastructure
  • Local architectural input vs. import-only design
This 2015 refresh keeps the course modern despite its 1990s-2000s origins.

Course Architecture

Layout

  • 18 holes
  • Par 72
  • 7,144 yards from black (championship) tees
  • 5,306 yards from red (forward) tees
  • Multiple tee boxes for varied skill levels (typically 4-5 tees)
  • 395 acres of land

Course Characteristics

  • Mountain backdrop on every hole
  • Emerald-green landscapes of mountain valleys and elevations
  • Challenging water hazards strategically placed
  • Strategic bunkers
  • Tree-lined fairways with mountain forest character
  • Elevation changes through the routing

Signature Hole — Par-3 14th

The course's defining hole is the par-3 14th:

  • Strategic challenge with tricky play
  • Visually striking with mountain backdrop
  • Memorable shot for any visitor
  • Photographic centerpiece of the round

Practice Facilities

The course offers comprehensive practice infrastructure:

  • Excellent driving range — full-size with multiple bays
  • Two greens for chipping and putting
  • Pre-round warm-up capabilities
  • Practice rounds can include driving range time

On-Course Amenities

  • Three halfway kiosks on the golf course
  • Food and cool drinks at each kiosk
  • Beverage service during round
  • Mid-round refresh without exiting course

The Modern Clubhouse

  • First-class locker rooms
  • Well-equipped pro shop
  • Large restaurant and bar
  • International and Thai cuisine
  • Course views from clubhouse seating
  • Tournament hosting capability

The Adjacent Resort — Chatrium 4-Star Hotel

Hotel Profile

  • Now under Chatrium Hospitality group management
  • 4-star designation
  • 47 rooms — boutique scale
  • Comfortable accommodation for overnight golfers
  • Course and mountain views
  • Restaurant + bar for non-golf-meals

Why Stay Onsite

The 3-hour drive from Pattaya makes same-day round-trip golf impractical:

  • Drive 3 hours to course
  • Play 4-5 hour round
  • Drive 3 hours back
  • Total 10-11 hour day — unsustainable
The on-site hotel solves this:
  • Arrive evening before — relax at hotel
  • Play full round next morning
  • Lunch and possibly second round in afternoon
  • Stay second night if multi-round trip
  • Return next day at leisure
This 2-day or 3-day golf trip format is the natural Chatrium experience.
