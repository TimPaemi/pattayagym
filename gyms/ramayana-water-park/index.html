Ramayana Water Park Pattaya

Overview

Ramayana Water Park is the largest water park in Thailand AND in all of Southeast Asia — a 184,000 square metre aquatic megaplex located 20 kilometres south of Pattaya at Na Jomtien, Sattahip, themed around the Ramayana — the great Hindu epic that's foundational to Thai culture and the source of the Ramakien (the Thai national epic). The park covers an area roughly equivalent to 25 football pitches — making it dramatically larger than the nearby Cartoon Network Amazone (Columbia Pictures Aquaverse) facility and putting it in a different scale category than virtually any other Asian water park.

The park opened officially on May 5, 2016 after construction beginning in 2011 — a 5-year build that allowed the developers to deliver 26 water slides across 34 lanes, a 600-metre-long lazy river that wraps around a natural hill with integrated waves, bubbles, and waterfalls, and a double wave pool with a 150-metre-wide beach that lets visitors swim with tubes in one section or wave-bathe without tubes in the other. The Ramayana cultural theming is integrated throughout the park architecture, landscaping, character costumes, and naming — distinguishing the experience from generic-themed water parks globally.

The international recognition is meaningful: in 2019, just 3 years after opening, Ramayana ranked #1 in Asia and #10 in the World among water parks per TripAdvisor Travellers' Choice Awards — confirming that the park's scale, ambition, and execution made it a genuinely world-class destination.

The Ramayana Theme

Ramayana / Ramakien Background

The Ramayana is one of the great epics of South and Southeast Asian literature:

  • Originated in ancient India — attributed to the sage Valmiki
  • Tells the story of Prince Rama, his wife Sita, his loyal brother Lakshmana, and the demon king Ravana
  • Adapted into Thai national epic as the Ramakien — Thailand's most important literary work
  • Kings of Siam took the name "Rama" — Thailand's monarchs are known as Rama I through Rama X (current king Rama X / Vajiralongkorn)
  • Foundational to Thai art, dance, literature, religion
  • Murals at Wat Phra Kaew (Temple of the Emerald Buddha) depict the entire Ramakien

How Ramayana Themes the Park

  • Architectural elements — Hindu/Thai-temple inspired structures
  • Landscaping — natural rock formations, jungle aesthetic
  • Character integration — hanuman (monkey general), demons, gods
  • Naming conventions — slides named after Ramayana characters and concepts
  • Live shows and entertainment — Thai dance and Ramayana-derived performances
  • Cultural significance — connects family entertainment with Thai cultural heritage
This deeper cultural theming is meaningfully different from Cartoon Network Amazone's commercial-IP approach.

The 26 Slides / 34 Lanes

Slide Breakdown

The 26 slides span 34 lanes (some slides are multi-lane):

#### Family-Friendly Slides

  • Tube rides for groups of 2-4
  • Body slides for teens and adults
  • Mat slides for racing-style competition
  • Splash zones for younger kids
#### Thrill Slides
  • High-speed drop slides for adrenaline seekers
  • Loop slides for the bold
  • Multi-lane racing slides — ride alongside friends and family
#### Family Tube Slides
  • Multi-rider tubes for shared experience
  • Variable-speed sections
  • Photo opportunities during descent

Slide Hardware

Like Cartoon Network Amazone, Ramayana likely uses premium international slide manufacturers (Polin Waterparks, ProSlide, WhiteWater West) for engineering-grade safety and ride quality.

The 600-Metre Lazy River

Why It's Distinctive

Most lazy rivers are simple circular channels around the perimeter of a water park. Ramayana's lazy river:

  • Runs 600 metres — substantially longer than typical
  • Wraps around a natural hill — provides elevation variation and scenic views
  • Integrated waves — periodic waves through the river course
  • Bubbles — bubbling sections for varied sensation
  • Waterfalls — ride through cascading water features
  • Natural-rock setting — feels like floating through a natural canyon rather than a constructed channel
This is one of the most ambitious lazy river installations globally — typically 200-400m is considered impressive, 600m is exceptional.

The Double Wave Pool with 150m Beach

Wave Pool Design

The wave pool is double-functioned:

  • Side 1: Tube section for floating wave-riding
  • Side 2: Open swim section without tubes for direct wave-bathing
  • Beach: 150 metres wide (490 feet) — among the largest artificial beaches in any water park

Wave Generation

  • Periodic wave generation — typically every 10-15 minutes for waves, between cycles for calm swimming
  • Wave heights: Range from gentle for children to substantial for adults
  • Family-friendly programming during off-peak intensity windows
This double design accommodates both casual swimmers and active wave-riders simultaneously.
