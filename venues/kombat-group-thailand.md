# Kombat Group Thailand

## Overview

Kombat Group Thailand is a **multi-discipline, all-inclusive, residential combat sports and fitness retreat** set on a peaceful rural campus in Huai Yai, just east of Pattaya. It is consistently ranked **5/5 on TripAdvisor with 409 of 416 reviewers giving it the maximum score** — making it one of the **most universally praised combat sports facilities in all of Thailand**. It was founded in **2004** by the late **Christian Daghio**, an Italian fight legend with **190 Muay Thai fights, 7 World Muay Thai titles, 2 WBA Asian Boxing titles, and the distinction of being the first Italian ever to fight and win at Lumpinee Stadium**. After Daghio's tragic death in 2018, his vision and operational ethos have continued under his trained team — and the camp is widely described in 2025-2026 reviews as honoring his original standards meticulously.

The facility is structured around a **residential, immersive model**: you book a package (1 week, 2 weeks, 1 month, longer), you live on the campus, three meals a day are prepared in the on-site kitchen, and your training, food, sleep, recovery, and community are all integrated into one experience. It is the **antithesis of a drop-in tourist gym** and the **best argument in Pattaya for the all-inclusive training holiday format**.

For travellers wanting **structure without the chaos of central Pattaya**, **multiple disciplines without resort-camp pricing**, and a **community that actively makes you better**, Kombat Group is the closest thing Pattaya has to a guaranteed-quality experience.

## The Founder — Christian Daghio (1969 – 2018)

To understand Kombat Group, you have to understand **Christian Daghio**. The camp is essentially the physical manifestation of one Italian fighter's quarter-century obsession with Thai combat sports.

### Coming to Thailand

Daghio arrived in Thailand at **age 21**, in the early 1990s, with the dream of becoming a professional Muay Thai fighter. The early years were brutal. His training conditions were primitive — for a stretch he was **literally training in an outdoor chicken coop**, sharing space with fighters who lived rougher than most Western athletes can imagine. He embraced it. Over the next 25+ years he built a fighting career that eventually made him one of the **most accomplished European fighters in the history of Thai combat sports**.

### Career Statistics

- **Muay Thai:** 190 fights, **145 wins** — including **7 World Muay Thai Championships**
- World titles in: **WAKO, WMA, WMF, WTKA, WKL** (Muay Thai sanctioning bodies)
- **First Italian to ever fight at Lumpinee Stadium** — and to win there
- **Western Boxing:** 35 matches (11 listed on BoxRec)
- **2× WBA Asian Boxing Champion**
- **2× ABF (Asian Boxing Federation) Champion**
- **1× WBF International Boxing Champion**
- **5 MMA fights**

He was, by the time he founded Kombat Group, one of the most credentialed European combat sports athletes alive.

### Founding Kombat Group (2004)

Daghio founded **Kombat Group in 2004** with a specific mission: to give "**farang**" (foreigners) the opportunity to **try and master Muay Thai inside an authentic but supportive environment** — bridging the gap between the no-frills, language-barrier-heavy traditional Thai camps and the resort-tourism Muay Thai vacations that were beginning to dominate the foreign market. The Huai Yai location was deliberate — far enough from central Pattaya to avoid distraction, close enough to remain accessible, set in lush gardens near Chak Nok Lake to provide the right atmosphere.

### The Tragedy — October 26, 2018

On **October 26, 2018**, Daghio stepped into the ring for what would be his **last fight** at **Rangsit Stadium in Bangkok**, facing the Thai fighter **Don Parueang** for the vacant **WBC Asia Silver Light Heavyweight Title**.

**Twenty seconds before the final bell of round 12**, after being knocked down and rising, Daghio collapsed in the ring — struck by a sudden **cerebral haemorrhage**, attributed by physicians to the **cumulative impact of his nearly 200 professional fights** across decades. He was rushed to hospital and emergency surgery was performed in record time. He remained in a coma. **Christian Daghio passed away on 2 November 2018** at age 49.

### Continuing Legacy

Daghio's sudden death was a profound loss for the global Muay Thai community and especially for the Italian / European combat sports world. Kombat Group did not close. Under his trained operational team — including longtime manager **Barbara** and head instructor **Cristiano** (Black Belt BJJ + Krav Maga + MMA) — the camp has continued operating without interruption. **Reviews from 2024-2026** repeatedly affirm that the standards, the philosophy, and the family-camp atmosphere remain unchanged. Daghio's fight memorabilia, photos, story, and ethos are deliberately woven into the camp's atmosphere. **You train in his shadow, but the camp does not feel like a memorial — it feels like an active, evolving organism.**

## Disciplines & Programs

Kombat Group is genuinely multi-disciplinary — broader than almost any other Pattaya camp:

- **Muay Thai** — flagship discipline, twice daily
- **Western Boxing** — proper boxing program with dedicated rings; rare in Pattaya outside this camp
- **Mixed Martial Arts (MMA)** — full curriculum, cage and mats
- **Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ)** — black belt instruction, Gi and No-Gi
- **Krav Maga** — practical self-defence, Israeli combat system
- **Weight Loss Bootcamp** — flagship non-fighter program; combines martial arts + functional fitness + nutrition
- **Total Fitness Bootcamp** — non-combat fitness program
- **Self-defence** — separate from Krav Maga, more general
- **Aqua Fitness** — pool-based conditioning
- **Beach Training** — outdoor sessions, occasional
- **Random outdoor activities** — built into longer-stay schedules

You can mix and match across disciplines. Many guests run a Muay Thai-as-flagship + BJJ-as-secondary combination. Krav Maga and MMA students often add boxing fundamentals.
