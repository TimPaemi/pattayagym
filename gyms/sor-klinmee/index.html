Sor Klinmee Muay Thai Gym

Overview

Sor Klinmee is a traditional, family-run Thai camp in the Nongprue area of Pattaya — the kind of authentic, no-frills, low-cost, deeply-rooted gym that built Muay Thai's reputation long before anyone marketed "training holidays" to foreigners. Founded in 2009 by Mit Klinmee (also known by his fighting name Tappaya Sit Or), the camp specializes in producing real Thai fighters and welcomes visitors who want to train inside that culture rather than at a gym designed around them. Pricing is among the lowest in Pattaya, training is authentic, and the atmosphere is unmistakably Thai.

The gym is also notable for being adjacent to Rambaa Somdet's M16 gym (built right next door in 2016 — Rambaa is family) and is part of a small concentrated cluster of authentic Thai camps in the Nongprue area.

History

Mit Klinmee, the founder, fought under the name Tappaya Sit Or during his career and was a champion in his era. After retirement he opened Sor Klinmee in 2009 to develop the next generation of Thai fighters. The camp has since produced several notable fighters and remains a working stable — fighters live and train on-site, foreigners train alongside them.

Notable Fighters

  • Sudsakorn Sor Klinmee — One of the more remarkable records in modern Muay Thai: 278 wins from 330 fights. Strategic, technical fighter; product of the camp's program.
  • Kongtappay Sor Klinmee — Mit's own son. Has fought in Pattaya stadiums in just six years of training.
  • Multiple other Thai fighters competing in regional and national circuits

Family Connection to Rambaa M16

Rambaa "M16" Somdet — Thailand's first MMA world champion (Shooto Flyweight, 2009) and Muay Thai legend — was born in Pattaya and trained at the original Sit Or camp from age 7. When Rambaa opened his own gym in early 2016, he built it 100 meters from Sor Klinmee. The two gyms remain closely linked and share trainers and culture — students often visit both.

Trainers

Authentic Thai trainers, many of them former pro fighters. English communication is more limited than at the tourist-facing camps (Fairtex, WKO) — though basic instruction in English is workable. Highly recommended for students who want real Thai pedagogy and are willing to bridge a small language gap for higher technical authenticity.

Daily Schedule

Standard Thai training rhythm — two-a-day, six days a week:

  • Morning session: Run + warm-up + shadow + padwork + bagwork + clinch
  • Afternoon session: Same structure with sparring days mid-week
  • Sundays: Rest

Pricing

This is where Sor Klinmee shines:

  • Single drop-in session: ~฿200-300 (~$6-9)
  • Monthly: estimated ฿4,000-7,000 (confirm at gym)
  • Privates: very affordable rates compared to tourist camps

This is roughly 1/3 to 1/4 of Fairtex's per-session pricing for genuinely comparable training quality (just less polished facilities and English support).

Facilities

  • One traditional Muay Thai ring
  • Heavy bags, banana bags
  • Open-air training pavilion (typical Thai camp design)
  • Basic free weights
  • Functional, no-frills

Accommodation

Limited on-site/nearby fan-room accommodation for serious students who want to integrate into the camp life. Rates are very modest. For travelers wanting more comfort, plenty of hotels in the Nong Prue / East Pattaya area within short taxi distance.

Location & Getting There

  • Area: Nong Prue, East Pattaya — quieter, less touristy zone
  • Nearby: Rambaa M16 Gym (next door, walking distance)
  • Easy by taxi/Bolt/songthaew from central Pattaya (~10-20 min depending on traffic)

Pros

  • Outstanding value — among the cheapest serious Muay Thai in Pattaya
  • Authentic family-run Thai camp culture
  • Strong fighter pedigree (Sudsakorn's record alone is exceptional)
  • Adjacent to Rambaa M16 — option to train at both
  • Real working stable, not a tourist showroom
  • Excellent for technique-focused students

Cons

  • Limited English — language gap can be an obstacle for first-timers
  • Basic facilities, no resort amenities
  • Less convenient location vs. central Pattaya gyms
  • Small online presence — most info from word of mouth

Reputation Summary

Held in high regard among serious Muay Thai students, especially European and American expats living in Pattaya, returning fight tourists, and anyone wanting depth over polish. Rarely featured in mainstream travel content, which actually contributes to its appeal — it's the gym you find when you stop reading marketing-heavy lists.

Best For

Serious fighters and committed students • Budget-conscious training travelers • Long-stay expats • Travelers wanting an authentic Thai camp without flying to small upcountry towns • Anyone interested in pairing Sor Klinmee with Rambaa M16 next door

Not Best For

Total beginners on a 1-week tourist holiday • Travelers needing strong English support • People who want pool, breakfast buffet, AC training hall
