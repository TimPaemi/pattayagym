# Editorial Style Guide

Pattaya Gym is useful first. Write like a local directory editor who has trained, booked, or checked the venue, not like a travel brochure.

## Voice

- Confident, specific, and practical.
- Expert but approachable.
- Plain English over slogans.
- British English for body copy: `centre`, `metre`, `traveller`, `programme`, `specialised`.
- Use Thai baht symbols and local area names naturally.
- Explain uncertainty directly: `verify current hours before travelling`, `confirm by phone`, `seasonal schedule`.

## Avoid

- Generic hype: `world-class`, `must-visit`, `hidden gem`, `best ever`.
- Unsupported rankings.
- Fabricated photos, reviews, prices, opening hours, or claims.
- Keyword stuffing.
- Overwriting official names to force British spelling.
- Removing useful detail just to make a page shorter.

## Venue Page Structure

A strong deep venue page usually includes:

- Clear overview of what the venue is and who it suits.
- Practical details: area, address, transport notes, phone, website, hours, price tier.
- Training or activity details.
- Who should choose it and who may prefer another option.
- Booking or first-visit advice.
- Nearby alternatives or related venues.
- FAQ entries generated by the build.

Keep headings descriptive. Use the venue name, category, neighbourhood, or user intent where natural.

## Deep Page Vs Stub

Deep pages:

- Have original Markdown body copy.
- Explain the venue in context.
- Include enough specifics for a visitor to decide whether to contact or visit.
- Avoid placeholders.
- Have a current `verified` date.

Stubs:

- Are generated only when a `data.js` record has no matching Markdown page.
- Should be temporary.
- Must not be allowed to replace deep pages silently.

The current production target is `158 deep + 0 stubs`.

## Frontmatter

Required fields:

- `id`
- `name`
- `category`
- `area`
- `verified`

Common optional fields:

- `address`
- `phone`
- `website`
- `social`
- `hours`
- `priceRange`
- `description`
- `tags`
- `mapsUrl`

The `id` must match the filename and `data.js` record.

## Categories

Use existing category keys from `data.js`. Do not invent a new key without adding it to `CATEGORIES` and updating generated pages intentionally.

If a venue fits multiple categories, choose the category that best matches the user's primary search intent. Use body copy and tags to surface secondary uses.

## Fact Checking

Before bumping `verified`:

- Check the venue website, Google Maps listing, Facebook page, or other public source.
- Confirm the exact public name.
- Confirm address or operating area.
- Confirm hours if published.
- Confirm phone format when present.
- Check whether price tier still matches the description.
- Record unresolved uncertainty in `CONTENT_AUDIT_2026-04-29.md` or a newer audit file.

Do not mark a venue verified just because the page was edited.

## Photos

Preferred image sources:

- Owner-supplied with permission.
- Venue official press/media kit with permitted use.
- Licensed stock images for category pages only, when clearly representative.

Do not use generated OG cards as venue photography. Do not scrape Google Maps, Facebook, or Instagram photos unless reuse rights are explicit.

## Cross-Linking

Internal links should help the reader choose between real alternatives.

Good examples:

- `compare Fairtex with Sityodtong`
- `beginner-friendly Muay Thai camps in Naklua`
- `nearby Jomtien yoga studios`

Avoid:

- Repeating the exact venue name every time.
- Linking every possible mention.
- Adding links that interrupt a sentence.

The build auto-links exact venue-name mentions. Editorial links can vary anchor text when they improve clarity.

## Titles And Descriptions

Generated pages should aim for:

- Title: roughly 50 to 60 characters where possible.
- Meta description: roughly 150 to 160 characters where possible.
- Include the primary intent, location, and brand without sounding mechanical.

Do not remove useful long-tail terms from body content to satisfy metadata limits.

## Tone Examples

Use:

```md
Fairtex is the polished North Pattaya choice for travellers who want Muay Thai, strength training, recovery, and accommodation in one compound.
```

Avoid:

```md
Fairtex is an amazing world-class destination that guarantees the best fitness experience in Pattaya.
```

Use:

```md
Budget travellers should ask about weekly passes before committing to a month, because Pattaya schedules often change between high season and low season.
```

Avoid:

```md
This gym has flexible pricing for everyone.
```
