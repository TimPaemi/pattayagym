★ About this venue

Pattaya's tourist economy is dominated by Walking Street energy, beach jet skis, and shopping malls — most visitors leave without seeing the real Pattaya hinterland: rural Thai villages, rubber and pineapple plantations, fishing communities, and the quieter back roads of Bang Lamung. Pattaya Bike and Boat Tours is the operator that has built its identity around taking small groups out of that tourist bubble and into the surrounding countryside on a bicycle.

Owner-operated and based on Pattaya-Naklua Road between Soi 18 and 18/1, the company runs guided bicycle tours on quiet roads that most tour operators never touch, plus optional boat extensions that pair cycling with sea-side village visits or quieter coastal coves. For active travellers wanting to actually see Thailand during their Pattaya trip — not just consume Pattaya's nightlife and beach — this is one of the strongest adventure-tourism picks in the city.

Position in Pattaya market

Pattaya's cycling landscape splits into:

  • Bike rental shops — Venture Bike Hire, Swiss Bike Pattaya, hotel rentals — equipment only, you ride solo
  • Cycling clubs — Cuore Masters, Monkey Xtreme, Jomtien Cycling, Pattaya MTB — local community group rides, mainly Saturdays/Sundays
  • Guided tour operators — Pattaya Bike and Boat Tours, RBB Pattaya, Colors of Pattaya — full guided experience with route planning, support, food
  • Specific guided tours — Sanctuary of Truth bike tour, etc. — single-route operators

Pattaya Bike and Boat Tours' specific position: multi-modal, operator-curated, off-tourist-trail. The bike+boat combination is unusual. Naklua location matters because it's already past the worst of Pattaya tourist density — tours start in a quieter zone and head further away from it.

What the tours look like

Typical structure (verify current offerings on their website):

  • Half-day guided rides — 2–4 hours
  • Quiet back-road routes through villages, farms, plantations
  • Boat extensions — visit islands, fishing villages, coastal stops
  • Small group format — typically 2–8 riders per tour
  • English-speaking guide throughout
  • Bikes provided — appropriate to the route (city bikes, mountain bikes)
  • Helmet, water, support van — standard inclusions
  • Snack / lunch stop at a local Thai eatery

The route specifics shift seasonally — wet-season tours pivot to dry-condition roads, dry-season tours can take more rural routes. The owner builds tours around what's actually fun to ride that week.

Bike rental (separate from tours)

Beyond guided tours, the company runs a bike rental side — useful for visitors who want to explore on their own but need a quality bike for a few hours or a few days. Mountain bikes, road bikes, city bikes, and beach cruisers are typically available. Hotel delivery often arrangeable.

Pros

  • Genuinely off-tourist-trail — see Thailand most tourists miss
  • English-speaking guides — easy for international visitors
  • Small groups — personal experience, not a 30-person bus tour
  • Bike + boat combinations — multi-modal day vs. single-discipline
  • Multiple difficulty levels — easy village rides through to longer routes
  • Equipment provided — show up empty-handed, ride
  • Naklua starting point — already quieter than central Pattaya
  • Long-running operator — know the area, the routes, the seasons

Cons

  • Not for hardcore road cyclists — these are leisure tours, not racing pace
  • Weather-dependent — wet-season tours may shorten or cancel
  • Booking required — not walk-in
  • Price higher than DIY rental — but you get guide, route, support
  • Limited distance — half-day tours, not multi-day expeditions
  • Naklua location — visitors staying in central Pattaya / Jomtien need to commute (10–15 min)

Best for

  • Active travellers wanting to see real rural Thailand from a bike
  • First-time Pattaya visitors who want a non-mainstream day
  • Couples or small groups wanting a curated outdoor experience
  • Travellers nervous about cycling solo on Thai roads
  • Photographers — village + boat + countryside content
  • Cyclists wanting a "rest day" pace ride during a longer Thai trip

Not best for

  • Competitive cyclists wanting Strava-segment hammer rides (try cycling clubs instead)
  • DIY explorers who already know Thai roads (rent solo from Venture Bike Hire)
  • Walking Street nightlife travellers — this is a daytime activity
  • Total cycling beginners on hilly routes — verify route difficulty matches your level

How to visit

The fastest path: book through their website (pattayabikeandboattours.com) or message PattayaBiking on Facebook with your dates, group size, and preferred tour type. Show up at the Naklua office on Pattaya-Naklua Road — Bolt/Grab will know the area; ask for "Ryan's Bar Irish Pub" as a landmark and walk a few doors.

Wear comfortable cycling shorts (or sturdy regular shorts), lightweight breathable shirt, real trainers (not flip-flops), sunscreen. Bring sunglasses and a water bottle (refills provided). Cash for tips and any extras at the lunch stop.

For travellers planning a multi-active day, pair the morning bike-and-boat tour with an afternoon at Wong Amat Beach (5 min from the Naklua office) for swimming, or with the Sanctuary of Truth if your tour doesn't already include it.
