Overview

Yoga Pattaya Studio bills itself as "the first dedicated international yoga studio in Pattaya" — and after years of operation it has emerged as the city's most established, comprehensive, and consistently top-rated yoga venue. Located on Thepprasit Soi 12, just minutes from Jomtien Beach, the studio offers a remarkably broad style range (Ashtanga, Mysore, Hatha, Hatha Vinyasa, Vinyasa Flow, Fitness Yoga, Stretching), trilingual instruction (English, Russian, Thai — reflecting Pattaya's international demographics), and small-group/private sessions designed to deliver real personal attention.

For travellers serious about a yoga practice during their Pattaya stay — and for the established expat community — Yoga Pattaya Studio is the default recommendation.

Position in the Market

Pattaya has multiple yoga studios (Yoga Haus, One-D Yoga, Nok Yoga, Ashtanga Yoga Pattaya, plus hotel/resort yoga programs), but Yoga Pattaya Studio is the longest-established, most international, and most reviewed. It's the one where you'll find the deepest stylistic range and the most reliable English instruction. Pricing is transparent, the booking flow is simple, and the studio caters to drop-ins as well as committed long-term students.

Yoga Styles Offered

A genuinely comprehensive lineup — rare for a single Thai studio:

  • Ashtanga Yoga — traditional structured series, daily practice
  • Mysore-Style Ashtanga — self-led practice with teacher adjustments (advanced Ashtanga format)
  • Hatha Yoga — foundational alignment-focused practice, beginner-friendly
  • Hatha Vinyasa Yoga — Hatha foundations with flowing transitions
  • Vinyasa Flow Yoga — dynamic flow practice
  • Stretching Yoga — recovery and flexibility focus
  • Fitness Yoga — energetic, fitness-oriented sessions
  • Private 1-on-1 sessions across all styles

Trainers

The studio specifies highly qualified, world-class instructors teaching small intimate groups and private sessions. Reviews repeatedly highlight:

  • Personalized attention — not just a workout class
  • Experienced instructors who scale poses and adjust hands-on
  • Welcoming atmosphere for absolute beginners as well as advanced practitioners
  • Trilingual capability — English, Russian, Thai depending on the class

Schedule

Daily classes — both morning and evening sessions throughout the week. Specific class times (which style, which day, which instructor) are published on yogapattaya.com/classes/. Drop-ins are welcome; advance booking via the website or WhatsApp/LINE is recommended for private sessions and Mysore practice.

Pricing

Transparent published pricing:

  • Single class drop-in: ~฿300-400 (typical Pattaya yoga rate)
  • Unlimited 2-week pass (any class): ~฿3,500
  • Unlimited 2-week pass (1-hour classes): ~฿2,900
  • Private sessions: premium rate, by appointment
  • Multi-week and monthly memberships: available

The 2-week unlimited at ฿3,500 is excellent value for a serious holiday practice — equivalent to ~฿250 per class if you attend daily.

Facilities

  • Spacious yoga studio with proper flooring and ventilation
  • Yoga props (blocks, straps, bolsters, blankets) provided
  • Mats available for rent or use
  • Showers / changing area
  • Small reception area with retail (yoga gear, mats)

The studio also operates an online yoga store through yogapattaya.com selling mats, props, and yoga apparel.

Online Yoga Store

Unique among Pattaya studios — they run a parallel e-commerce yoga store on their site shipping yoga gear across Thailand. Worth knowing if you need to replace a mat or buy props mid-trip.

Location & Getting There

  • Area: Thepprasit Soi 12 — same general area as Venum Training Camp
  • Walking distance to Jomtien Beach (~5-10 min depending on exact end of soi)
  • Easy access from Sukhumvit Road via Thepprasit
  • Songthaew or short taxi from central Pattaya

Reputation Summary

Top-rated yoga studio in Pattaya across TripAdvisor and most local "best of" lists. Particularly strong reputation among:

  • Long-stay expats who treat it as their home studio
  • Serious yoga travellers wanting Ashtanga or Mysore practice
  • Russian-speaking visitors (large demographic in Pattaya — the Russian-language instruction is a real differentiator)
  • First-time yoga travellers wanting a structured intro in English
