Overview

One-D Yoga Studio (also stylized OneD Yoga) is a modern, prominent yoga studio along the Pattaya-Naklua Road offering a comprehensive multi-style yoga curriculum spanning Hatha, Vinyasa Flow, and Yin yoga alongside private sessions, workshops, and retreat programs. The studio sits at 571/32 Moo 5, Naklua 16/2, Pattaya-Naklua Road — placing it in the same Naklua corridor as Nok Yoga Pattaya but with a meaningfully different programming emphasis.

Where Nok Yoga emphasises traditional Thai stretching and self-healing yoga, where Yoga Pattaya Studio focuses on Ashtanga + Mysore + multilingual international yoga, where Yoga Haus pushes Hot Yoga + Fit Ball fitness intensity, and where Balance Yoga integrates sound healing wellness, One-D Yoga Studio occupies the "modern accessible multi-style" middle ground — offering the mainstream curriculum that appeals to the broadest practitioner base without strong tilt toward any specific tradition.

The studio's strengths are its full membership flexibility (drop-in, monthly, long-term packages), private session availability, workshop programming, and retreat options — the full menu of services that committed yogis expect from an established studio. Combined with clean modern facilities, knowledgeable teachers, and the Naklua location's quieter character, One-D delivers a comfortable home for daily yoga practice.

The Three Core Yoga Styles at One-D

Hatha Yoga

The foundational traditional yoga style:

  • "Hatha" in Sanskrit means "force" — referring to the physical practice
  • Slower-paced postures with longer holds
  • Breath-and-posture coordination
  • Classical alignment focus
  • Beginner-accessible
  • Builds foundation for all other styles
  • 45-75 minute classes typical

Hatha is the entry point for most yoga practice — accessible, gentle progression, foundational for understanding alignment and breath.

Vinyasa Flow

The dynamic flowing style:

  • "Vinyasa" in Sanskrit means "flow" or "to place in a special way"
  • Postures linked by breath — each movement matches an inhale or exhale
  • Sun salutations as core sequence
  • Continuous movement rather than static holds
  • Cardiovascular component — heart rate elevates
  • Creative sequencing by teacher — varies class to class
  • 60-75 minute classes typical

Vinyasa is more energetic and modern — appeals to practitioners who want yoga that feels like a workout while still meditative.

Yin Yoga

The slow, deep, contemplative style:

  • "Yin" is the Chinese taoist concept of passive, cool, internal
  • Long passive holds — 3-5 minutes per posture
  • Floor-based poses primarily
  • Targets connective tissue — fascia, joint capsules, ligaments
  • Meditative quality — focus on sensation and breath
  • Excellent for stress reduction
  • Complement to active practice — balances fast-paced styles
  • 75-90 minute classes typical

Yin is the perfect counterbalance to Vinyasa or Hot Yoga — same students often practice both for balance.
