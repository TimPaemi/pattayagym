Pickleball is the fastest-growing sport on the planet, and Pattaya — with its enormous, mostly-50+ expat retiree base — has become one of the largest, most active pickleball scenes in Thailand. **Pickleball Pattaya** on Pratumnak Soi 6 is the city's flagship dedicated facility: courts built specifically for pickleball, top-tier lighting for evening play, full equipment included, and beginner lessons from an English-speaking licensed coach. If you've never picked up a paddle, this is the easy on-ramp. If you already play and just need a court to drop into, this is the obvious answer.

## Position in Pattaya market

Pattaya does have several multi-sport racquet venues that share their courts with pickleball — **Fitz Club** (Royal Cliff, premium tier with brand-new CoolTop multi-purpose court), **Pattaya Tennis & Badminton Inter Club** (2 hard courts with permanent pickleball lines, both indoor and outdoor), and a handful of condo-resort communities painting lines onto their tennis courts. Those are great if you're a member or guest already, but they're shared infrastructure — booking windows are tight, court time competes with badminton and tennis, and the players on adjacent courts don't always understand pickleball culture.

**Pickleball Pattaya is different**: it's purpose-built for the sport. No fighting tennis players for time, no shared lines confusing your eye, no hunting for paddles. You roll up, the courts are exactly the right size, the nets are at exactly the right height, the lighting is engineered for the speed of a wiffleball, and everyone on site is there for the same reason as you. This is what dedicated pickleball venues feel like in the US/Canada — and it's now the standard in Pattaya thanks to this club.

## Location — Pratumnak Hill

Pratumnak Hill sits between Central Pattaya and Jomtien, north of the Pattaya/Jomtien border. It's the upmarket residential corridor — high-rise condos, hotel-condo hybrids, view-property developments. **Pratumnak Soi 6** is one of the quieter sois on the hill, well off the chaos of Walking Street and Beach Road, with relatively easy parking.

For visitors: it's a short Bolt/Grab ride from anywhere in Central Pattaya or Jomtien. From Central Festival Beach Mall it's about 10–12 minutes. From Jomtien Beach it's 8–10 minutes. From the Bali Hai Pier (where many ferry day-trippers land) it's 6–7 minutes uphill.

For residents: huge swathes of long-stay expats live within 5 minutes of Pratumnak Soi 6 (whole streets of condos like The Cliff, The View, Cosy Beach View, Pratumnak Hill condos), which is exactly why the venue thrives — pickleball is built around walk-up, drop-in, twice-or-three-times-a-week social play, and Pratumnak gives the sport its catchment.

## Programming

The venue runs the typical drop-in / open-play / lesson rhythm familiar to pickleball players globally:

- **Beginner lessons** — 1-on-1 or small group with the in-house licensed coach. Standard format: 60–90 min, paddle/ball/footwork basics, dink drills, soft game introduction. Most adults can play a real (slow, patient) game by the end of session 2.
- **Open play** — sessions where players show up, paddles go in a rack, you rotate through games with whoever's there. The natural format for new arrivals to meet other Pattaya players.
- **Ball-machine practice** — solo drilling, especially good for beginners working on serve / return / third-shot drop without needing a partner.
- **Private bookings** — block a court for your group (couples / families / friends) at non-busy hours.

## The Pattaya pickleball scene

Pattaya has one of the **fastest-growing pickleball communities in Thailand**, particularly anchored by the 50+ expat retirement crowd. The sport is famously easier on knees and shoulders than tennis or badminton, the games are short, the social-to-effort ratio is excellent, and most players are within 10 years of each other in age — which the Pattaya retirement demographic finds magnetic. It's normal to see Brits, Aussies, Americans, Canadians, Germans, Scandinavians, and Russians all rotating through the same evening session, with Thai locals mixed in.

Active scene markers worth knowing about:

- Regular **senior tournaments** and social leagues
- Mixed doubles, men's, women's, age-bracket events
- WhatsApp / Facebook groups for ad-hoc games and ride-shares
- Periodic visiting coaches and clinics from Bangkok / international circuits

If you're new to Pattaya and want a shortcut to a friend group inside your first week — get on a paddle and show up to open play. The community absorbs new players rapidly.

## Cons

- **Limited court count** vs. larger US/Canada-style mega-clubs — peak hours fill up fast
- **Outdoor surface** means weather can interrupt (April–October monsoon season)
- **Pricing higher** than free public tennis courts — but expected for dedicated facilities
- **Pratumnak parking** can get tight on peak weekends
- **Niche sport** — if you want tennis or badminton, this is not your venue (try Fitz Club or Inter Club instead)

## How to visit

The fastest path: message Pickleball Pattaya through their Facebook page or website to book a court or a beginner lesson. Show up at the venue with workout-friendly shoes (court shoes ideal — not running shoes with high heel drops; the lateral movement in pickleball is hard on running soles). Paddles, balls, water, and the rest are sorted on site.

For travellers: it's a 5–15 minute Bolt/Grab from any Pattaya hotel. Tell the driver "Pratumnak Soi 6 — Pickleball Pattaya". For residents: the easiest way into the community is to book one beginner lesson, then ask the coach when the next open-play session
