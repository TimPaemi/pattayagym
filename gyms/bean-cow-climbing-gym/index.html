Overview

Bean Cow is Pattaya's family-friendly climbing destination — a 500-square-metre purpose-built climbing facility in Huay Yai with bouldering, top rope, and lead climbing, plus dedicated kids play areas, free water, showers, and a community-center culture that distinguishes it from the more downtown-tourist-focused Deep Climbing Gym at Harbor Mall. The gym positions itself as "come for the climbing, stay for the community" — and the operational details (weekly bouldering wall resets, free first-timer orientation, family pricing tiers, kids zones, multi-month visit passes) all reinforce that ethos.

For Pattaya residents wanting a regular climbing community to belong to, families wanting kids climbing programs, and travellers seeking a more relaxed alternative to the mall-floor Deep Climbing, Bean Cow is the answer. Pattaya now has two indoor climbing options — Deep is the first and mall-located option; Bean Cow is the community-driven option. Active climbers in Pattaya often visit both.

Facility — 500 sqm of Climbing

The 500 square metre facility includes:

  • Bouldering area with frequently-reset problems
  • Top rope wall — main height climbing
  • Lead climbing routes — clip-as-you-go with full lead-rated rope work
  • Dedicated kids play areas — multi-generational use
  • Equipment rental included with day passes
  • Free water for climbers
  • Showers for post-climb cleanup

Weekly Bouldering Reset

A defining Bean Cow feature: a section of the bouldering wall is reset every week. This is meaningful for regular climbers — it means the routes you climbed last week are different from the routes available this week, keeping the bouldering challenge fresh and progressive. Weekly reset cadence is rare in small climbing gyms and signals serious operational commitment.

Pricing

Bean Cow has a transparent multi-tier pricing structure documented at beancowclimbing.com/rates:

Day Passes

  • Day pass (adult): ~$15-15.36 USD (~฿520-540)
  • Includes: climbing access + general areas + kids play areas
  • Equipment rental (shoes, harness): may be additional or included depending on package

Multi-Visit Passes

  • 5-visit pass: valid for 3 months
  • 10-visit pass: valid for 6 months

Multi-visit passes give climbers flexibility for irregular visit cadence — useful if you climb 1-2x per week, not daily.

Memberships

  • 1-month pass: unlimited entry for 30 days, includes rental shoes
  • Annual pass: unlimited entry for one year, includes rental shoes, non-transferable

The annual pass for committed local climbers is the value tier — especially with rental shoes included.

First-Timer Experience

Bean Cow is unusually first-timer welcoming:

  • Free 10-minute orientation with safety lesson
  • One free harness rental
  • Free belay from staff for your first session

This free intro is more generous than most Pattaya-area gyms. Combined with the community-center ethos, it makes Bean Cow especially friendly for absolute beginners.

Adult Climbing Test Requirement

Adults wanting to climb the rope wall must pass a belay test to operate independently. If you don't pass:

  • Schedule a class with a Bean Cow instructor (CWA-certified) to learn proper belaying
  • Hire a belayer to climb with for the session

This is a standard climbing-gym safety practice and reflects Bean Cow's commitment to safety culture.

Schedule

Note: Closed Mondays.

  • Tuesday-Thursday: 15:00-22:00 (afternoon-evening hours)
  • Saturday-Sunday: 10:00-20:00 (full-day weekend hours)
  • Friday: confirm at gym
  • Monday: closed

The Tuesday-Thursday afternoon-only schedule is unusual — most climbers come on weekends or evenings. The schedule reflects the gym's community-event programming model rather than tourist-walk-in-anytime.

Community Programming

  • Kids climbing programs — multiple age tiers
  • CWA-certified teachers — Climbing Wall Association credentials
  • Top rope safety classes — proper belay-test prep
  • Bouldering competitions — periodic events
  • Special events — birthdays, group bookings, summer camps

The "Community Center" in the gym's name is genuine — Bean Cow operates as a gathering place for the local climbing community rather than just a commercial climbing wall.
