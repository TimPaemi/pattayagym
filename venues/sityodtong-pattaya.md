# Sityodtong Pattaya

## Overview

Sityodtong is **arguably the most historically significant Muay Thai gym in the world**. Founded in **1960** by the late Grandmaster **Kru Yodtong Senanan** (1937-2017), the camp has produced over **57 world champions** in six decades of operation, including some of the most legendary names the sport has ever known. Where Fairtex offers polish and resort-style logistics, Sityodtong offers **lineage**, **authenticity**, and a direct line to old-school Thai training philosophy. For purists and pilgrims, this is *the* destination.

The camp moved from its original Rayong location to a larger site in Naklua, Pattaya, around 1975, and has operated continuously since. The vibe remains genuinely Thai — open-air ring, dirt floor in places, fighters living on-site, no-frills accommodation, and a quiet seriousness that contrasts with the more polished tourist-facing camps.

## History

Kru Yodtong opened the original Sityodtong in **Mabtaput, Rayong province**, in 1960. As the camp's reputation grew through the 1960s and into the early 70s, he relocated the operation to a larger plot in **Naklua, Pattaya**, around 1975, where it remains today. Yodtong was inducted into multiple martial arts halls of fame and is widely regarded as one of the architects of modern Muay Thai pedagogy. He passed away in 2017 at age 80; the camp continues under his trained successors and family.

The Sityodtong name has expanded internationally — affiliate gyms in Boston (USA), Los Angeles, Clearwater (FL), and elsewhere — but the Pattaya headquarters is the original and the only one with the direct lineage and original ring.

## Notable Alumni

The Sityodtong roll call is breathtaking:

- **Samart Payakaroon** — 4-time Lumpinee champion, considered by many the greatest Muay Thai fighter ever, also WBC Super Bantamweight world boxing champion
- **Kongtoranee Payakaroon** — Samart's brother, multi-division Lumpinee champion
- **Daotong Sityodtong** — Lumpinee champion
- **Chatchai Paiseetong** — World champion
- **Yoddecha Sityodtong** — Multi-time champion
- **Rob Kaman** (Netherlands) — Trained extensively under Yodtong; one of the godfathers of European kickboxing
- **Ramon Dekkers** (Netherlands) — Spent significant time at Sityodtong; widely considered the greatest non-Thai Muay Thai fighter in history
- **Mark DellaGrotte** — American who trained at Sityodtong, went on to become one of the top MMA striking coaches in the world (Joe Lauzon, Kenny Florian, Marcus Davis)

## Trainers

Today's coaching staff are former Sityodtong fighters and Yodtong's direct students. Reviews repeatedly highlight the trainers' patience with beginners, attention to fundamentals, and willingness to correct technique rather than just running you through cardio. English ranges from functional to fluent depending on the trainer.

## Daily Schedule

Six days a week, two sessions per day:

- **Morning:** 08:00-10:00 — run, shadow, padwork (one-on-one), bagwork, clinching
- **Afternoon:** 15:00-17:30 — same structure, with mid-week sparring

Beginners are scaled down; fighters get harder rounds, sparring blocks, and ring time. Sundays closed.

## Pricing

Considerably more affordable than the resort-style camps:

- **Drop-in single session:** ~฿400 (~$12)
- **Monthly membership:** ~฿7,000 (~$213)
- **Private 1-on-1:** ~฿600 per hour
- **Weekly packages:** available — confirm at reception

This pricing reflects the authentic-camp model: less polished facilities, but you're paying for direct access to lineage and trainers, not amenities.

## Facilities

- One traditional Thai ring
- Heavy bags, banana bags, uppercut bags
- Open-air training pavilion (some shaded, some not)
- Basic free-weight area
- Simple changing rooms

The facility is functional, not flashy. This is what an old-school Thai camp looks like.

## Accommodation

On-site fan rooms and AC rooms available for visiting students and fighters at modest rates (typically ~฿200-600/night fan, ~฿500-1,500 AC depending on availability and length of stay). Walking distance to local markets and beach. For more comfort, plenty of hotels within a 5-10 min taxi.

## Location & Getting There

- **Area:** Naklua, north Pattaya — walking distance to Naklua market and Wong Amat Beach
- **Easy by:** taxi, Bolt, songthaew (baht bus). Sukhumvit Road access nearby.
- Free informal parking on-site

## Pros

- Unmatched lineage and historical importance
- Authentic training, real Thai feel
- Strong reputation for technique-focused coaching
- Affordable pricing — among the best value in Pattaya
- Excellent for fighters who want a quieter, less touristy environment

## Cons

- No-frills facilities — no pool, no resort amenities
- Accommodation is basic
- Less English hand-holding than tourist-oriented camps
- Open-air training means hot, humid, and exposed to weather

## Reputation Summary

Considered a **pilgrimage destination** by serious Muay Thai practitioners worldwide. The combination of Yodtong's legacy, the alumni list, and the still-active authentic training environment make it unique. Reviews universally praise the trainers and warn that this is a serious gym — not a vacation gym.

## Best For

Serious students, fighters, and Muay Thai purists • Anyone wanting an authentic Thai camp experience • Travelers comfortable with basic accommodation • People who want to train under direct Sityodtong lineage

## Not Best For

Pure beginners on a tourist holiday looking for resort vibes • Travelers who need AC, pool, breakfast buffet • Anyone uncomfortable with open-air, traditional facilities
