Sanit Sport Club is one of East Pattaya's anchor multi-purpose sports facilities — a **two-zone operation** combining a comprehensive **fitness centre** with a **dedicated Muay Thai gym**, set near the scenic **Mabprachan Reservoir** in the residential expat belt of East Pattaya. Owner-operated by **Sanit Sirirup**, the club has grown to **1,000+ members** since establishment and serves a mix of long-stay Pattaya expats, Thai locals, and visiting Muay Thai students.

Most Pattaya Muay Thai camps are pure-discipline operations — you go for Muay Thai and only Muay Thai. Most Pattaya commercial gyms are pure fitness — cardio, weights, machines, no martial arts. Sanit Sport Club's distinctive position is **integrating both into a single membership** — you train Muay Thai with a 40-year Lumpinee-veteran trainer in the morning and lift weights / do cardio in the afternoon, all on one tab, all in one location.

## The Muay Thai zone

Head trainer **Kru Rung — Rungsawad Lukpintong** — brings 40 years of training and competing at **Lumpinee** (Bangkok's most prestigious Muay Thai stadium) and **Rajadamnern** (the second great traditional stadium). That's not marketing — that's a generational lineage credential. Kru Rung's career trajectory put him on the same canvas as the legendary Thai champions of the 1980s–2000s; he now passes that experience to Sanit's current students.

The current standout fighter is **Krasuk Sanitmuaythaigym** — a **MAX Muay Thai champion** currently competing abroad. MAX Muay Thai is one of Thailand's largest professional promotions; producing a current titleholder is a real credential for the camp's coaching pipeline.

### What Muay Thai training looks like

Standard Pattaya-camp format:

- Skip / shadow / warm-up
- Bag work (8-10 rounds, 3 min each with 1 min rest)
- Pad rounds with trainers (4-6 rounds)
- Clinch / sparring
- Conditioning / cool-down

For total beginners, expect the trainers to scale work appropriately. For competitive fighters, expect serious sparring partners and Krasuk-tier game-planning.

## The fitness zone

The fitness centre side of Sanit serves the membership's broader needs:

- Cardio (treadmills, bikes, ellipticals)
- Strength machines
- Free weights
- Functional training space
- Group classes — spinning, TRX, yoga, aerobics on rotating schedules

The fitness side is **commercial-tier** — well-suited to general fitness goals, fat-loss, basic strength training, and recovery on Muay-Thai-rest-days. It's not a powerlifting cathedral (that's Muscle Factory's role), and it's not a boutique HIIT studio (that's IG Center). It's a solid, member-friendly gym that compounds nicely with the Muay Thai side.

## Location — Mabprachan Reservoir / East Pattaya

Mabprachan Reservoir sits in **East Pattaya / "Darkside"** — the residential belt east of Sukhumvit Road. Quieter, more local-expat than tourist, popular with long-stay Western and Russian residents. The 4 km running loop around the reservoir is one of Pattaya's best free running routes — pair it with a Sanit membership for a full daily fitness routine.

For visitors: 15–25 min from central Pattaya by Bolt/Grab. For residents: most East Pattaya / Mabprachan / Khao Talo / Soi Siam Country Club residents are within 5–10 min by scooter.

## How to visit

The fastest path: message **sanitsportclub** on Facebook to confirm the current Muay Thai schedule, drop-in pricing, and address pin. Show up for a session in workout gear; trainers will assess your level and pair you appropriately. Bring hand wraps and mouth guard for Muay Thai sessions.
