★ About this venue

Cho Nateetong Thai Boxing Camp

Overview

Cho Nateetong Thai Boxing Camp is an authentic budget-tier Pattaya Muay Thai camp on Sukhumvit Soi 34 — a working fighter operation that combines a standard weight-lifting gym at the front with a dedicated Muay Thai training area at the back, offering drop-in pad sessions at ~฿270 per session. The combined weight + Muay Thai layout is meaningful: students can do strength training before or after pad work without changing venues, and the trainers give a good amount of time on the pads rather than rushing through quick rounds.

Cho Nateetong sits in the same authentic-Pattaya tier as Sor Klinmee, Sitpholek, and the Klinmee family cluster — non-tourism-focused working camps where Thai trainers run sessions at accessible prices for serious students. The Sukhumvit 34 location is East Pattaya / Darkside — accessible from East Pattaya residential areas, condos along Sukhumvit, and a short songthaew ride from central Pattaya tourist hotels.

For Pattaya residents and students seeking affordable authentic Muay Thai training without the resort-tier polish or the multi-discipline expansion of newer gyms, Cho Nateetong delivers traditional pad work at a price point dramatically below the resort camps (Fairtex ฿800/drop-in, Silk ฿300-600, Kombat Group ฿800+).

The Sukhumvit Soi 34 Setting

Geographic Context

Sukhumvit Soi 34 is in East Pattaya / Darkside:

  • Sukhumvit Road — Pattaya's main north-south arterial
  • East side of city — residential expat belt
  • Less tourist density than Walking Street area
  • Songthaew accessible along Sukhumvit
  • 15-25 minute drive from Walking Street / Beach Road

Why The Location Works

For Cho Nateetong's customer base:

  • East Pattaya residents — convenient walk-up
  • Long-stay expats in Sukhumvit corridor condos
  • Russian / European residents in the area
  • Less tourist-pricing pressure — keeps session rates accessible

The Combined Gym + Muay Thai Layout

What's Distinctive

Most Pattaya Muay Thai camps are pure Muay Thai facilities — open-air pavilions with rings, bags, and pad-holding stations. Few integrate a proper weight room.

Cho Nateetong's layout:

  • Front: Standard weight-lifting gym
    • Free weights — dumbbells, barbells
    • Machines — typical commercial gym equipment
    • Open to general gym members likely
  • Back: Dedicated Muay Thai area
    • Ring + heavy bags + pad-holding zones
    • Mat space
    • Combat-sport-specific equipment

This two-zone layout lets students train in both modalities at one venue without changing locations — meaningful for strength + striking cross-trainers.

Training Format

Standard Muay Thai Session

A typical pad session at Cho Nateetong:

  • Warm-up (5-10 minutes) — light skipping, shadow boxing
  • Pad work (3-5 rounds with trainer)
  • Bag work (2-3 rounds)
  • Clinching (where appropriate)
  • Cool-down stretch
  • Total session: 60-75 minutes typical

Trainer Approach

Reviews specifically mention: "the trainers give you a good amount of time on the pads" — meaningful contrast to some Pattaya camps that rush sessions to maximize student turnover.

The Cho Nateetong style appears to be:

  • Patient pad work — trainers don't rush
  • Technical correction during rounds
  • Drop-in friendly — no long-term commitment required
  • Both casual and committed students supported
