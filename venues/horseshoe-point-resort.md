# Horseshoe Point Resort & Country Club

## Overview

Horseshoe Point Resort & Country Club is **the largest, most institutionally credentialed, most multi-disciplinary equestrian and adventure sports facility in Thailand** and one of the leading riding schools in all of Asia. Spread over **1,500 acres of green countryside** near **Mabprachan Reservoir** in East Pattaya, the property combines **a serious international-standard equestrian operation** (100+ horses across rare breeds, 5 indoor and outdoor arenas, dressage, polo, classical riding, full curriculum from kids' pony rides to advanced trail) with an **adventure-sports playground** (paintball, zipline, mountain biking, archery, water games, multi-sport field) and an **on-site resort with accommodation, dining, and event facilities**.

For travellers in Pattaya wanting **anything to do with horses** — from a 15-minute first-time pony ride for a child to an intensive multi-week classical riding clinic — Horseshoe Point is essentially the only serious answer. It's also the city's strongest **family activity destination**, with paintball, zipline, and adventure programming sitting alongside the riding stables.

## History & Pedigree — Why This Place Is Different

Most "horse riding" attractions in tourist regions are basic trail-ride operations with a small handful of horses. **Horseshoe Point is a different category of facility entirely.** The **International Riding School** was established **in 1999** and has grown into one of **Asia's leading riding schools**, with a deliberate focus on **classical and dressage instruction** alongside recreational riding.

The school holds two formal credentials that put it in a different league:

- **Thailand Equestrian Federation (TEF) certification** — the national governing body's stamp of approval as a serious equestrian institution
- **International affiliation with Centro Equestre da Leziria Grande (Portugal)** — the school of **Maestro Luis Valença Rodrigues**, one of the world's leading instructors in the **Art of Classical Riding**. This is genuinely rare: a Pattaya facility holding institutional ties to one of Europe's premier classical equestrian academies

This combination — Thai national accreditation plus Portuguese classical-riding lineage — makes Horseshoe Point a credible **destination for serious dressage and classical-riding students from across Asia**, not just a tourist photo-op.

## Location & Land

- **Address:** 100 Moo 9, Pong, Bang Lamung District, Chonburi 20150, Thailand
- **1,500-acre estate** in the green countryside east of Pattaya
- **Adjacent to Mabprachan Reservoir** — the area locally known as "Lake Mabprachan", a recreational watershed area popular with cyclists and runners
- **Distance to Pattaya Beach:** 14.5 km (~9 miles) — about 25-30 min by car
- **Distance to Jomtien Beach:** 14.7 km (~9.1 miles)
- **1 minute** from Three Kingdoms Park
- **8 minutes** from Mabprachan Lake recreation area
- **10 minutes** from Siam Country Club golf

The location is deliberate — **far enough out of central Pattaya to host a 1,500-acre estate, close enough that day visitors can drive in for a couple hours of activities.**

## The Equestrian Operation

This is the crown jewel of the property and the reason it earns the "category of its own" description.

### The Horses (100+)

Horseshoe Point keeps **over 100 horses across multiple breeds** — a herd size that puts it ahead of any other Thai equestrian facility:

- **Lusitano** — Portuguese classical riding breed (reflecting the Centro Equestre affiliation)
- **Appaloosa** — American spotted breed
- **Lipizzaner** — the famous Spanish Riding School breed; one of Europe's most prestigious classical horses
- **Arabs (Arabian)** — endurance and trail
- **Warmbloods** — the standard sport-horse breed for dressage and show jumping
- **Falabella** — miniature horse breed (popular for kids' programs)
- **Thai pony** — local breed, often used for beginner and pony rides

The presence of Lusitanos, Lipizzaners, and Warmbloods specifically signals a serious classical/sport-horse operation rather than a trail-ride business.

### The Arenas

**Five arenas** (mix of indoor and outdoor) accommodate different disciplines and weather:

- Indoor arenas — used during heat and rainy season
- Outdoor jumping/dressage arenas
- Polo field
- Multipurpose training arenas

### Programs Offered

The riding school operates with **full curricula for all levels**:

- **Pony rides for kids** — 15-minute introductory rides on small ponies and Falabellas
- **Beginner lessons** — basic seat, balance, walk/trot in arena
- **Intermediate lessons** — canter work, basic jumping, fundamental dressage
- **Advanced lessons** — show jumping, advanced dressage
- **Classical riding** — the school's specialty (Centro Equestre lineage)
- **Polo lessons and play** — polo facilities and clinics
- **Trail rides** — from short countryside loops to **2-hour experienced-rider trails**
- **Pony Eventing Classes & Open Competitions** — the property hosts youth eventing championships (e.g. Pony Eventing Class & Open July 28-30, 2023)
- **Riding holidays / camps** — multi-day intensive programs

### On-Site Tack Shop

Full-service equestrian retail — saddles, bridles, helmets, riding boots, breeches, gloves. Useful for visiting riders who didn't bring full kit, and for resident riders refreshing equipment.

## Adventure Sports & Family Activities

Beyond horses, Horseshoe Point operates a **wide-ranging adventure sports program** — making the facility a full-day family destination rather than only a riding school:

- **Paintball** — both individual shooting and full team games. Multiple courses
- **Zipline & Adventure Park** — high ropes, zipline circuits, suitable for kids and adults
- **Mountain Biking** — bike rentals + trails through the 1,500-acre property
- **Archery** — traditional archery range
- **Water Balls** — inflatable water-walking spheres on the property's water features
- **Multi-purpose Sports Field** — football, frisbee, group activities
- **Children's playground** within the resort grounds

### 2012 Indicative Pricing (Updated rates not on website)

The property does not publish current activity prices online. **2012 indicative rates** (2026 will be higher — confirm with property):

- **Water Balls:** ~฿100/person
- **Mountain Bikes:** ~฿50/bike/hour
- **Paintball:** ~฿200 (shooting) or ~฿350 (team game)
- **Archery:** ~฿300/person for 10 arrows

**Horse riding and polo lesson rates** vary widely by program, instructor level, and duration — confirm at the riding school reception.

## Accommodation, Dining & Events

Horseshoe Point operates as a full **resort & country club**:

- **On-site accommodation** — hotel rooms and villas spread across the property
- **Multiple restaurants and dining venues**
- **Country Club facilities** — pool, lounges, communal spaces
- **Wedding & event venues** — the property hosts large-scale events, including weddings, corporate retreats, and equestrian competitions
- **Wedding Pavilion** — purpose-built event space

For multi-day equestrian visitors or families on a Pattaya holiday wanting an alternative to beach hotels, the on-site stay turns the property into a self-contained destination.

## Pricing Model

Three pricing tiers depending on use case:

### Day Visitor

Pay-per-activity pricing. Family of 4 doing 1 hour riding + paintball + lunch can spend **฿2,000-5,000** total depending on selections.

### Multi-Day Equestrian Visitor

Riding holiday packages combine **accommodation + multiple lessons + meals + ranch activities**. Typical 3-day/2-night packages start ~฿8,000-15,000/person depending on lesson intensity and accommodation tier.

### Membership / Long-Stay

For Pattaya residents who want regular riding access, the school offers **lesson packages** (e.g. 10-lesson cards) and **stable-board** for owners boarding their own horses. Confirm with riding school directly.

## Reviews & Reputation

Held in **strong regard among Pattaya families and equestrian visitors**. The riding school is consistently named **the best equestrian facility in Pattaya and one of the best in Thailand**. Notable strengths in reviews:

- **Quality of horses** — well-cared-for, varied breeds, suitable for every level
- **Instruction quality** — TEF-certified instructors, classical-riding pedigree
- **Range of activities** — riding alongside paintball/zipline makes it a family-friendly all-day destination
- **Property scale** — the 1,500 acres genuinely feels like a country estate, not a tourist photo location
- **Helmet and safety culture** — proper equipment, attentive staff

Some reviews note that the **resort hotel side** is inconsistent in quality (some rooms dated, some excellent) — the equestrian side is the consistent star.
