Camps with accommodation.

By TimPaemi · 6 min read · Updated 2026-06-02 · How we rank →

Stay-and-train packages remove logistics excuses — eight verified Pattaya paths from budget guesthouse pairing to 5-star resort camp.

Pattaya · 157 venues hand-checked · Compare gyms

Stay-and-train packages remove every logistical excuse to not train. Pattaya has multiple Muay Thai camps offering on-site accommodation — from budget bunkhouse to 5-star resort. This guide maps verified options from our directory: what each tier includes, who it suits, and what to ask before you wire a deposit.

Hub: Training holiday · best Muay Thai ranked · East Pattaya camps · compare.

Pricing tier reference

  • ฿: Under ฿15,000/week — basic shared accommodation, training included, no frills
  • ฿฿: ฿15,000–30,000/week — private room, meals, training, basic amenities
  • ฿฿฿: ฿30,000–60,000/week — resort-tier, pool, premium gym, mixed disciplines
  • ฿฿฿฿: ฿60,000+/week — 5-star resort with Muay Thai integration, spa, multi-sport

1. Fairtex Training Center Pattaya — flagship resort camp

Fairtex Pattaya is the largest stay-and-train camp in Thailand. On-site hotel, 25m pool, restaurant, 9 rings, 17 trainers. All-inclusive: room, meals, two daily Muay Thai sessions, pool, gym. ฿฿฿ to ฿฿฿฿ depending on room class. Visa-support letters available for long stays. Naklua side — area guide.

Best for: first-time visitors who want resort experience with zero logistics. Champions train alongside tourists.

2. Kombat Group Thailand — all-inclusive multi-discipline

Kombat Group in Huai Yai (East Pattaya) is the most fully all-inclusive option. Daily two-a-days Muay Thai + optional BJJ, MMA, boxing. Includes airport transfer, accommodation, meals, training, laundry, pool, restaurant. ฿฿฿. Best for 1–4 week trips testing multiple disciplines.

3. Pattana Sports Resort — golf + Muay Thai + fitness

Pattana Sports Resort (Bo Win, ~30 min from central) is multi-sport — golf, tennis, pool, fitness, Muay Thai program. Hotel accommodation. ฿฿฿. Best when your group includes non-fighters (kids, partners, golfers).

4. Horseshoe Point Resort — equestrian + Muay Thai combo

Horseshoe Point integrates Muay Thai into a larger resort. East Pattaya. ฿฿฿. Unusual but works for horse-riding plus combat sport — equestrian guide.

5. Sanit Sport Club (Mabprachan) — long-stay-friendly

Sanit Sport Club pairs training with nearby guesthouses within walking distance of Mabprachan Lake. Less polished than resorts but cheaper and flexible (฿). Best for 1+ month stays — East guide.

6. Hilton Pattaya — hotel-first, Muay Thai as amenity

Hilton Pattaya on Pratamnak Hill: 5-star fitness with PT-led Muay Thai add-on. ฿฿฿฿. Not for learning from scratch — excellent for maintaining training on a luxury stay.

7. Andaz Pattaya Jomtien — 5-star fitness with Muay Thai access

Andaz Pattaya Jomtien — Hyatt resort, full fitness, on-call Muay Thai trainers. ฿฿฿฿. Pick over Hilton if Jomtien beachfront matters — Jomtien guide.

8. Movenpick Siam Na Jomtien — resort + sport amenities

Movenpick Na Jomtien has sports center and arrangements with nearby Muay Thai gyms. ฿฿฿฿. Family-friendly — family sport.

DIY stay-and-train (not on-site hotel)

WKO (~฿4,000/month training) plus Jomtien or central guesthouse (฿4,000–8,000/month) beats half the "all-inclusive" weekly price if you handle logistics. Day passes for strength days. Best for independent travellers who hate resort bubbles.

What to actually ask before booking

  • Is the price all-inclusive? Meals, laundry, airport transfer may be extra — get itemized quotes.
  • Sessions per day included? Two-a-days standard at serious camps; resorts sometimes one-a-day.
  • Visa letter? Fairtex and Kombat provide support for ED pathways — confirm with Pattaya Visa Help.
  • Cancellation policy? Often 7–14 days notice; low season sometimes flexible.
  • Skill level expectation? All welcome beginners; fighter-track gyms need honest disclosure.

FAQ

What's the cheapest camp with accommodation in Pattaya?

WKO + nearby guesthouse often totals ฿8,000–12,000/month — less than half many all-inclusive weekly rates. You manage housing yourself.

How long should I book?

First-timers: 1–2 weeks. Serious trainees: 4–12 weeks. Never done Muay Thai? Book 1 week extendable on arrival.

Are visas required?

Under 30 days: visa exemption for many passports. 30–60 days: tourist visa. 60+ days: ED/sport pathways — training & visa guide.

What's included in "all-inclusive"?

Typically room, meals, two MT sessions, pool/gym. Usually NOT massage, personal gear, Bangkok trips, alcohol. Confirm per camp.

Can my non-training partner stay?

Fairtex and Kombat accommodate partners; Hilton/Andaz/Movenpick are hotels first. Sityodtong case-by-case.

Are camps open year-round?

Yes. Peak Nov–Mar; rainy season quieter. Songkran (mid-April) many gyms close briefly.

Resort camp vs DIY guesthouse — decision matrix

Choose resort stay-and-train when you want one invoice, airport pickup, meals handled, and you are okay with camp social bubble culture. Choose DIY when you want Jomtien café life, dating, remote work, or switching gyms mid-month. Fairtex and Kombat optimize the first; WKO + condo optimizes the second.

Room class questions

  • AC vs fan room — Pattaya humidity makes AC worth it for sleep quality if you two-a-day train.
  • Shared vs private bath — shared is fine for budget fighters; couples should confirm private bath availability.
  • Fridge and desk — remote workers should confirm before paying monthly resort rates.
  • Pool hours and guest policy — some camps restrict pool to training guests only before 18:00.

Meal reality on all-inclusive

Camp kitchens rotate Thai staples — rice, protein, vegetables. Western portion expectations differ. Allergies (gluten, dairy, halal) need email confirmation — do not assume on arrival. Vegetarians survive but may need supplement snacks from Tesco Lotus near Jomtien or central.

Partner and family on camp property

Non-training partners at Fairtex/Kombat are common — confirm extra bed fees. Kids at resort camps (Movenpick, Centara links) pair with kids sport guide. Partners who hate fight sport should not book rural east camps without their own transport — entertainment is training-adjacent.

Songkran, Loy Krathong, and holiday closures

Thai public holidays may shorten sessions or close kitchens midday. Songkran (mid-April) closes many gyms 2–3 days — book around it. Christmas/New Year peak fills resort rooms — deposit early Nov if you fly Dec.

Deposit and payment norms

Serious camps request 30–50% deposit to hold peak-season rooms — bank transfer or card, rarely PayPal. Get written confirmation listing sessions per day, meal count, and room class. Scams are rare at listed venues but common on Facebook copycat pages — book only through contacts on our venue pages or official websites linked from compare.

On arrival walk the facility before signing multi-week extensions: ring count, shower hot water, Wi-Fi for remote work, and distance to 7-Eleven. If the gym oversold capacity, negotiate downgrade or partial refund before you sleep night one.

Film a short walk-through video on check-in day — useful if room class does not match booking photos and you need evidence for a polite downgrade request at reception.

Laundry: resort camps include it; DIY guesthouse athletes use coin laundry near Mabprachan — budget ฿40–60 per load and afternoon drying time in rain season.
