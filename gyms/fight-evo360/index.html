★ About this venue

Fight EVO360 Muay Thai Gym

Overview

Fight EVO360 is a performance-structured Muay Thai gym on Thepprasit Road that distinguishes itself from traditional Pattaya Muay Thai camps by integrating modern strength & conditioning, performance nutrition, and structured athletic development with traditional Muay Thai instruction. The gym positions itself with the explicit philosophy of "evolving the athlete within every person ready to challenge their limits in the gym or in life" — a more modern fitness-and-development orientation than the lineage-pure Thai camps (Sor Klinmee, Sityodtong, Petchrungruang, Sitpholek) that emphasise traditional Muay Thai progression.

The gym is located at 193/15 Moo 11, Thepprasit Road, Muang Pattaya, Bang Lamung District, Chonburi 20150 — placing it in the Thepprasit corridor that connects central Pattaya, Jomtien, and East Pattaya, accessible from multiple residential and tourist zones. Training operates Mon-Sat with morning classes 08:00-10:00, evening group classes, and open gym sessions — matching the morning + evening rhythm typical of serious Pattaya Muay Thai facilities.

The trainer roster includes elite and championship-level Muay Thai and boxing trainers, and the curriculum spans well-developed and battle-tested programs in Muay Training, Strength & Conditioning, and Performance Nutrition. For students wanting modern athletic-development structure layered on top of authentic Muay Thai instruction, Fight EVO360 fills a meaningful niche in the Pattaya combat sports market.

The Performance-Structured Philosophy

Beyond Traditional Muay Thai

Where most authentic Thai camps focus narrowly on Muay Thai technique, conditioning, and sparring, Fight EVO360 layers in:

  • Modern strength & conditioning programming
  • Performance nutrition guidance
  • Athletic development metrics (likely strength, mobility, recovery tracking)
  • Periodization of training cycles
  • Goal-specific programming for fight prep, fitness, weight management

This is the contemporary Western model of combat sports training — informed by sports science, periodization theory, and performance analytics.

Why This Matters for Foreign Athletes

Foreign athletes (especially those with prior strength training, CrossFit, or sports backgrounds) often want:

  • Familiar training language alongside Muay Thai
  • Strength + Muay Thai integration rather than pure striking focus
  • Nutrition support for body composition goals
  • Structured progression with measurable outcomes
  • Contemporary pedagogy rather than purely "do what the Thai trainer does"

Fight EVO360's positioning addresses this demographic specifically.

Trade-Offs

The performance-structured approach means:

  • Less "soul of Thai Muay Thai" vs. traditional family camps
  • More gym-like structure vs. open-air pavilion training
  • Higher emphasis on metrics vs. instinctive coaching
  • Western training-science influence alongside Thai technique

For some, this is the appeal. For others (lineage purists), it's a dilution.

The Trainers — Championship-Level

The gym is staffed by elite and championship-level Muay Thai and boxing trainers. Specific credentials are typically published on the gym website but verified at the gym:

  • Active or recently retired Thai pro fighters as trainers
  • Boxing trainers alongside Muay Thai specialists
  • English communication workable for foreign students
  • Hands-on padwork instruction
  • Sparring partner availability

The "championship-level" claim aligns with the standard Pattaya pro-camp trainer recruitment — most Pattaya gyms try to retain trainers with stadium/world-title experience, and Fight EVO360 follows this model.

Programs

Muay Thai Training

Core curriculum:

  • Traditional 8-limbs Muay Thai — punches, kicks, knees, elbows, clinch
  • Padwork, bagwork, sparring, clinching
  • Conditioning runs outside the gym
  • Fight preparation for students competing
  • Beginner accessibility with technique-first progression

Boxing Training

  • Western boxing curriculum
  • Hand technique focus — useful for Muay Thai students wanting cleaner punches
  • Boxing-specific padwork
  • Cross-trains with Muay Thai effectively

Strength & Conditioning

  • Modern S&C programming — unusual at traditional Thai camps
  • Olympic lifts, deadlifts, squats as foundation
  • Sport-specific conditioning for Muay Thai athletes
  • Periodized training cycles

Performance Nutrition

  • Diet planning for fight prep, weight cuts, body composition
  • Macronutrient guidance
  • Supplementation guidance when appropriate
  • Hydration protocols for hot-weather training

Open Gym Sessions

  • Self-directed training between scheduled classes
  • Equipment access without formal class
  • Recovery / mobility sessions
  • Light technique work

Schedule

Mon-Sat operation:

  • Morning classes 08:00-10:00 — primary group session
  • Evening group classes — second daily session
  • Open gym — self-directed access between scheduled times
  • Sundays — typically rest or open gym only

The morning + evening rhythm is standard for serious Muay Thai facilities — replicates the pattern of authentic Thai camps.

Location — Thepprasit Road

Geographic Setting

Thepprasit Road is a major arterial connecting central Pattaya to Jomtien and the broader Eastern Seaboard:

  • 193/15 Moo 11 specifically — accessible from multiple residential zones
  • 15-25 minutes from Walking Street / Beach Road
  • 10-15 minutes from Jomtien Beach
  • 15-25 minutes from East Pattaya / Mabprachan area
  • Songthaew (baht bus) accessible along Thepprasit
  • Free parking typical for Thepprasit gyms

Why Thepprasit

The Thepprasit corridor serves a mix of Thai residential and expat populations — a less tourist-saturated zone than Walking Street area, with more authentic Thai daily life character while still accessible.

Pricing

Per gymdesk.com pricing structure typical:

  • Drop-in single class: ~฿400-600
  • 1-month morning Muay Thai: ~฿4,000-6,000 (typical Pattaya tier)
  • 1-month all-access (morning + evening + open gym): ~฿6,000-10,000
  • Private 1-on-1: premium rate
  • Personal training (S&C): premium rate
  • Performance nutrition consultation: add-on package
  • Multi-month commitments: progressive discounts

Fight EVO360 is mid-tier performance-oriented pricing — meaningfully more expensive than budget hostel-attached camps (Pattaya Thai Boxing ฿300/session), comparable to other authentic mid-tier Pattaya camps.
