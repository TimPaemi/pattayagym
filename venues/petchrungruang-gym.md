# Petchrungruang Gym

## Overview

Petchrungruang is one of Pattaya's **most beloved authentic family Muay Thai camps** — a small, traditional gym that has been quietly producing fighters since **1986**, and which gained worldwide reputation as **the home gym of Sylvie von Duuglas-Ittu**, the American Muay Thai fighter, journalist, and author of the most-read Muay Thai blog in the English-speaking world. Where larger camps run training as a business operation, Petchrungruang runs it as a family — three generations of the Petchrungruang family work the camp daily, including the founder, who at over 70 years old still gives technical advice ringside.

For students who want the **real, slow-cooked, generationally transmitted Muay Thai experience** — and don't mind that it comes wrapped in a small alley off Sukhumvit Road rather than a marble lobby — this is one of the most authentic options in all of Thailand.

## History

The gym was founded in **1986** by **Bamrung Petchrungruang**. Now in his 70s, Bamrung is still actively present at the camp, sharing technical insights with students and fighters. The camp has been continuously operated by the family for nearly four decades, transitioning naturally between generations without losing its identity.

The current head trainer is **Kru Nu** — a former **Lumpinee champion** and the family member who runs daily padwork sessions and oversees the fighter development.

## Sylvie von Duuglas-Ittu — The Camp's Most Famous Resident

**Sylvie von Duuglas-Ittu** is one of the most fascinating figures in modern Muay Thai. An American who relocated to Thailand in 2012 and made Petchrungruang her home camp, she has accumulated **over 250 professional Muay Thai fights** — making her **the most-fought female Muay Thai fighter in history** by a significant margin. She fights under the name **Sylvie Petchrungruang**.

Beyond fighting, Sylvie is the founder of **8limbsus.com / Sylvie Study**, a documentary project capturing oral histories, technique studies, and footage of legendary Thai fighters and trainers. Her work has made Petchrungruang globally visible to the English-speaking Muay Thai community. Visitors often discover the camp through her writing.

## Trainers

- **Kru Nu** — Lumpinee champion, head trainer, runs padwork in the matted training area
- **Bamrung Petchrungruang** — founder, 70+, still ringside and technically active
- Additional family members and former fighters round out the coaching staff
- Strong **emphasis on kids program** — the camp has a thriving Thai kids fighter pipeline (one reason for the early 5 AM session)

## Daily Schedule

Unique three-session-a-day rhythm to accommodate the kids team:

- **Early morning (~05:00):** Kids' team session — adults can join for run + light work
- **Morning (~08:00):** Adults' session — shadow, padwork, bagwork, clinch
- **Afternoon (~15:30):** Main adult session with sparring days mid-week
- Mon-Sat; Sundays rest

The 5 AM session is unusual for Pattaya camps and reflects the kids' Thai school schedule. Foreign students typically attend morning + afternoon.

## Pricing

Among the **most affordable serious training in Pattaya**:

- **Drop-in single session:** ~฿200-400
- **Monthly unlimited:** estimated ฿4,000-7,000 (confirm at camp)
- **Private 1-on-1:** affordable Thai-camp rates

Pricing reflects the family-run, low-overhead, no-marketing-budget reality of the camp. **You're paying for time with Lumpinee-champion trainers at a fraction of resort-camp rates.**

## Facilities

- One Muay Thai ring
- Matted weight training area used for padwork
- Heavy bags
- Open-air training pavilion
- Basic but functional equipment

The facility is small and traditional. This is a **fighter-development gym, not a fitness facility**. Reviews describe it as feeling like a "family gym" — small, intimate, no separation between visiting students and the resident fighters.

## Accommodation

Modest on-site or near-site accommodation available for committed students at very affordable rates. Most visiting foreigners arrange their own nearby housing, which is plentiful in this part of South Pattaya.

## Location & Getting There

- **Area:** South Pattaya, off Sukhumvit Road
- **Specifically:** Soi Sukhumvit-Pattaya 50, after the "Heavy Duty" building, near the Paint Ball Park
- Easy access by taxi/Bolt/Grab; songthaews along Sukhumvit Road
- Quieter than central Pattaya but still very accessible

## Pros

- Authentic family camp culture — three-generation deep
- Lumpinee-champion head trainer (Kru Nu)
- Globally recognized through Sylvie's blog/documentary work
- Among the lowest pricing for high-quality training
- Strong technique focus — not a tourism-driven camp
- Welcoming to long-stay students and serious learners
- Small intimate vibe — quick personal attention

## Cons

- Limited English — Thai-language environment dominates
- Basic facilities, no pool/sauna/AC
- Small camp — less variety of training partners than big resort camps
- Hard to find online without prior knowledge
- Best suited for students who can commit weeks/months, not weekend tourists

## Reputation Summary

Held in **near-mythic regard among serious foreign Muay Thai practitioners** — particularly women fighters (because of Sylvie's pioneering work), long-stay expats, and English-language Muay Thai journalists. Repeatedly described in reviews as "the real thing," "an authentic Thai family gym," and "what Muay Thai used to be everywhere before tourism reshaped most camps."

## Best For

Serious students and committed long-stay fighters • Women fighters following Sylvie's path • Travelers prioritizing authenticity over comfort • Anyone interested in deep technical immersion under family-camp culture

## Not Best For

Tourist-style 1-week training holidays • Students who need strong English instruction • Travelers wanting modern fitness amenities • Anyone uncomfortable with small, traditional training environments
