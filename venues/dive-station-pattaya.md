# Dive Station Pattaya

## Overview

Dive Station Pattaya is **one of Pattaya's SSI-certified dive operations** — a smaller-scale dive shop offering **full-day diving programs for both first-timers and certified divers**, plus **snorkeling trips for non-divers wanting to experience the Pattaya near-island reefs without the certification commitment**. Where Pattaya's premium PADI operators (No Limit Divers, Real Divers, Aquanauts, Mermaid's) compete on **PADI 5-Star credentials and instructor pipeline depth**, Dive Station occupies **the SSI tier** — Scuba Schools International, PADI's main competitor and the world's second-largest dive certification body.

The format is straightforward: **first-timers get a confined-water briefing followed by open-water dives**, **certified divers join 2-tank boat trips**, and **non-diving family members can join the same boat for snorkeling**. **Equipment hire is included** for SSI certification courses, removing first-time-diver gear-fit concerns.

For Pattaya divers comfortable with **SSI certification (functionally interchangeable with PADI for most practical purposes)** and wanting **a smaller-operation experience without the IDC career-track polish of premium operators**, Dive Station offers an accessible entry into Pattaya scuba.

## What SSI Is

### SSI vs. PADI

**Scuba Schools International (SSI)** is **the world's second-largest dive certification body** after PADI:

- **Founded 1970** (vs. PADI 1966)
- **3,000+ dive centers globally** (vs. PADI's 6,500+)
- **Headquartered in Stuttgart, Germany** (vs. PADI's Florida)
- **More popular in continental Europe** (Germany, Netherlands, Italy, Spain)
- **Less common in US/UK** where PADI dominates

### Practical Equivalence

For recreational divers, **SSI and PADI certifications are functionally interchangeable**:

- **Same skill standards** at equivalent levels (SSI Open Water = PADI Open Water)
- **Same depth limits** (18m for OW, 30m for AOW, 40m for Deep Specialty)
- **Mutually recognized** by most dive shops globally
- **Reciprocal** — SSI-certified diver can rent gear at PADI shops and vice versa

The real difference is **brand recognition** — PADI is more familiar to English-speaking tourists, SSI to European continental tourists.

### SSI Advantages

- **Cheaper** courses typically (lower licensing fees)
- **More flexible curriculum** delivery
- **German headquarters** — strong continental European fit
- **Digital learning materials** more accessible
- **Lower per-course costs** for students

### When SSI Makes Sense

- **Continental European visitors** more familiar with SSI
- **Budget-conscious students** seeking lower per-course costs
- **Casual recreational divers** not pursuing professional certification

## Course Programs

### Discover Scuba Diving (DSD) — First Timer

- **Half-day or full-day** intro program
- **Pool / confined water briefing** first
- **2 open water dives** on actual reefs
- **No certification** — just the experience
- **Equipment provided**
- **Cost:** ~฿3,500-4,500

### SSI Open Water Course

- **3-4 day full certification**
- **Theory + confined water + open water dives**
- **SSI Open Water Diver certification** valid worldwide
- **Equipment hire included**
- **Cost:** ~฿13,000-16,000

### Full-Day Certified Diver Trip

- **2 boat dives at near-island reefs**
- **For divers with existing certification** (any body — PADI, SSI, NAUI, BSAC, etc.)
- **Group trips with mixed divers**
- **Lunch on boat typically**
- **Cost:** ~฿2,000-2,800

### Snorkeling Trip

- **For non-divers** or family members of divers
- **Same boat as diving trip**
- **Snorkel + mask + fins + life vest** provided
- **Surface-only experience** at coral reefs
- **No certification needed**
- **Cost:** ~฿800-1,500
