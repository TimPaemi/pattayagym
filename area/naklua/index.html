01 About this neighborhood

What Naklua / North Pattaya is for.

Naklua is everything north of the Naklua-Pattaya bay line — the original fishing-village end of Pattaya before the city expanded south. It still feels different: narrower streets, more Thai-owned shops, a quieter beach (Wong Amat), and fewer tourists per square metre than Central Pattaya.

For Muay Thai trainers specifically, this is the most-loaded neighborhood in Pattaya — Fairtex, Sityodtong, and several smaller authentic camps all train here. Long-stay couples, female trainers, and tourists looking for a calmer base often pick Naklua over the noisier south.

02 Best for

When to pick Naklua / North Pattaya.

01

// Muay Thai (especially long-stay)

Fairtex resort camp, Sityodtong (1960 legacy), Petchrungruang family camp — three of Pattaya's most-respected Muay Thai gyms within a few km of each other.

02

// Beach + quiet

Wong Amat Beach is calmer than Pattaya Beach. Good for families with kids and for trainers who want to recover by the water.

03

// Resort training

Mid-upper hotels (Cape Dara, Centara Grand Mirage) cluster here with full fitness/spa amenities.

03 Transport & access

How to get there.

Naklua Road is the main north-south spine; the baht-bus from Central Pattaya runs the length of it. North Pattaya Road connects to Sukhumvit. Bus station Pattaya Nuea is in this area, serving Bangkok-bound minivans and buses.

04 Landmarks & orientation

Where you are.

  • Wong Amat Beach (quiet family beach)
  • Sanctuary of Truth (carved wooden temple)
  • Lan Po Naklua market (Thai-style fresh market)
  • Mum Aroi seafood pier
  • Naklua bus terminal (Bangkok routes)
05 Starter pick

If you're new here.

If Muay Thai is the trip, start at Fairtex or Sityodtong depending on whether you want the resort experience or the lineage experience. Stay at one of the Naklua hotels for walkable training + beach + market food access.

04 Common questions

Naklua / North Pattaya — FAQ.

Is Naklua a good base for training?

Naklua, at the north end of Pattaya, is quieter and more residential than the centre - a good choice if you want a calmer base while staying close to the city. It is home to one of the largest Muay Thai resort camps in the area, so it suits a focused training stay.

What sports is Naklua best known for?

Naklua headline is Muay Thai - it hosts a major resort camp - alongside fitness venues and some wellness and yoga. The Wong Amat beach end is the quieter, more upmarket stretch of the North Pattaya coast.

How do I get around Naklua?

Naklua Road runs north from Central Pattaya and is on a baht-bus route, so getting into the centre is cheap and easy. The area is fairly compact; a motorbike helps for venues spread along the coast.

Who is Naklua best for?

Visitors who want a quieter, more residential base than Central Pattaya but still close to it - particularly Muay Thai trainees who want resort-style training without nightlife on the doorstep.
