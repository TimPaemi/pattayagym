Pattaya Monkey Hash House Harriers

Overview

Pattaya Monkey Hash House Harriers ("Monkey Hash" or "PMH3") is the longer-form, more demanding sister kennel of the foundational Pattaya Hash House Harriers — a Hash House Harriers chapter that runs only on odd-numbered months (January, March, May, July, September, November) on the 3rd Saturday, with each run requiring participants to walk at least 8 kilometres over moderately difficult terrain in approximately 2 hours. Where the Pattaya Hash House Harriers (PH3) runs every Monday with a mainstream 5-10 km mixed-pace format suitable for runners, joggers, and walkers, Monkey Hash filters for the more committed segment of the Pattaya hashing community — those who want longer routes, harder terrain, and a more dedicated 6-times-per-year monthly tradition.

The bi-monthly schedule (every other month) and the explicit minimum 8km / 2-hour requirement mean Monkey Hash is fundamentally different in character from typical hashes. Most HHH chapters globally welcome casual walkers and slow joggers. Monkey Hash sets a clear bar: you need to be capable of moderate hiking-level activity for 2+ hours over varied Thai countryside terrain.

For the Pattaya hashing community, Monkey Hash provides the deeper-engagement option alongside PH3's weekly mainstream gathering and Pattaya Jungle Hash's Sunday-jungle-focus offering. For visiting hashers from international kennels who happen to be in Pattaya during a Monkey Hash weekend, the run is a memorable Thai countryside adventure of meaningful athletic substance.

The Bi-Monthly Schedule

Frequency

  • Every other month (odd-numbered months only)
  • January, March, May, July, September, November
  • Six runs per year
  • Skips even-numbered months (February, April, June, August, October, December)

Run Date

  • Always the 3rd Saturday of the run month
  • Predictable scheduling — easy for members to plan
  • Longer interval than PH3's weekly format = more anticipation per event

Why Bi-Monthly?

The bi-monthly schedule reflects:

  • Smaller, more dedicated membership — not casual weekly drop-ins
  • Longer planning for harder routes and terrain
  • Special-event feel — each run has more buildup
  • Parallel without competition to PH3 — different days, different formats

The 8km Moderate-Difficulty Format

What "8km Moderate Terrain in 2 Hours" Means

The minimum standard:

  • 8 km — meaningfully longer than typical hash runs (5-10 km range)
  • Moderate terrain — hills, trails, varied surfaces (not pavement-only)
  • 2 hours — implies ~4 km/hour pace (walking speed for hilly terrain)
  • Walk at least — running encouraged but not required for the harder sections

Implications

  • Fitness baseline required — couch-to-hash newcomers will struggle
  • Hiking shoes recommended — running shoes inadequate for Thai countryside
  • Hydration critical — 2 hours in tropical heat
  • Self-pacing skill needed — managing energy across 2 hours
  • Trail experience helpful — reading terrain, picking lines

Comparison to PH3

  • PH3 typical: 5-10 km, mixed terrain, accommodates walkers + slow joggers
  • Monkey Hash: 8+ km, moderate terrain, 2-hour minimum, fitter participants
This is a different self-selection — Monkey Hash filters out the casual walker.

Run Format

Pre-Run

  • Meet at the announced location (varies)
  • Hare(s) for the day pre-set the trail with paper / chalk markings
  • Hash fee typically ฿200-400 for visitors (covers run + drinks + snacks + meal)
  • Sign in — visitors and returning members
  • Briefing on trail and any specific safety concerns

The Run

  • Hare's chosen route through varied terrain
  • Trail markings (dots, checks, false trails)
  • Pack moves through trail following markings
  • 8km minimum with possible longer sections
  • Moderate terrain including hills, trails, possibly water crossings
  • Pace varies by participant fitness and terrain

The Circle

After the run, the standard hash circle ceremony:

  • Beer and snacks
  • Songs and down-downs
  • Hash naming for new members
  • Religious Advisor (RA) runs proceedings
  • Visitors recognized if international

Post-Circle

  • Group meal at local restaurant or BBQ
  • Continued socializing into evening
  • Strong social bonding typical of hash culture

Run Locations

Monkey Hash runs in varied locations across the Pattaya extended region:

  • East Pattaya farmland
  • Khao Mai Kaew area
  • Sattahip hills
  • Bang Saray countryside
  • Buddha Hill jungle trails
  • Hilltop forest reserves
  • Coastal cliffside trails
The terrain selection prioritises moderate-difficulty trails — meaning the Hare seeks out routes that provide the 8km+ challenge.

Participant Profile

Who Joins Monkey Hash

  • Long-stay expat residents of Pattaya
  • Fit hikers and trail walkers
  • Visiting hashers from international kennels
  • Repeat PH3 members wanting harder challenges
  • Pattaya Jungle Hash participants rotating between groups

Demographics

  • 30+ years old typical — younger members rare
  • Western (British, Australian, German, Scandinavian, American) dominant
  • Both genders participate — typical Pattaya hash demographic
  • Long-term Pattaya residents — short-stay tourists rare at Monkey Hash given infrequent schedule
  • Fitness-aware members who have experienced PH3 first

Pricing & Membership

Run Fee

  • ~฿200-400 per run for visitors
  • Includes: trail use, drinks at circle, snacks, meal
  • Higher fee than PH3 typical due to bi-monthly format

Annual Membership

  • Combined PH3 + Monkey + Jungle memberships sometimes available
  • Annual fee typically ฿500-1,500
  • Member discount on per-run fees

Pros

  • More demanding format for committed hashers
  • 8km guaranteed minimum — substantial athletic outing
  • Bi-monthly = special-event feel
  • Strong member bonding through shared challenge
  • Varied terrain across Pattaya extended region
  • Filters for fitness — likeminded participants
  • Established hash culture — songs, traditions, ceremony
  • Visiting hasher friendly — IKO-style international cred
  • Saturdays = different from PH3's Mondays — additive not competitive

Cons

  • Bi-monthly schedule — only 6 runs per year
  • 8km minimum filters out casual walkers
  • Moderate terrain demands fitness and equipment
  • 2-hour duration in tropical heat is challenging
  • Less social density than weekly PH3
  • Visitors may not align with Pattaya stay dates
  • Bawdy hash culture may not suit conservative travellers
  • Trail terrain unpredictable — bring shoes ready for mud/hills

Best For

  • Fit Pattaya residents wanting bi-monthly hiking challenge
  • PH3 graduates wanting harder format
  • Visiting international hashers during odd-month 3rd Saturday
  • Trail-walking enthusiasts appreciating varied terrain
  • Long-stay expats building Pattaya outdoor community
  • Fitness-aware mid-life adults

Not Best For

  • Casual walkers — PH3 better fit
  • First-time hashers — start with PH3 to learn culture
  • Short-stay tourists — schedule rarely aligns
  • Pure runners seeking pace — try standard running clubs
  • Very fit speed athletes — moderate terrain limits pace
  • Mobility-limited travellers — terrain is demanding
