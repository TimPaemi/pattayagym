★ About this venue

The Royal Varuna Yacht Club is Thailand's most prestigious sailing institution — founded over 60 years ago, anchored on Pratamnak Hill in Pattaya, and the spiritual home of Thai yacht racing. Where most Pattaya watersports operators run rentals, lessons, and tourist-package experiences, Royal Varuna is a real members-only yacht club in the British / Commonwealth tradition: clubhouse, beach bar, swimming pool, boat storage, repair facilities, and an institutional racing calendar that draws world-class fleets to Pattaya every Jan–Mar.

For Pattaya travellers who actually sail — whether they own a boat back home, raced as juniors, or want to take their first RYA-certified sailing or powerboat course — this is the venue.

RYA-accredited training

Royal Varuna's most accessible offering for non-members is its RYA (Royal Yachting Association) training programme — internationally-recognised certifications across:

  • Sailing (dinghy, keelboat, cruising)
  • Powerboating (PB1, PB2, intermediate, advanced)
  • Junior sailing (kids' programmes)
  • Race coaching (for advancing competitive sailors)
RYA certificates are globally portable — useful if you want to charter a yacht in the Mediterranean or Caribbean later, captain a powerboat back home, or build credentials toward a yachtmaster ticket.

Regatta calendar (Jan–Mar peak)

Pattaya's prime sailing season is the cool-dry NE-monsoon Nov–Mar window, with the peak regatta calendar concentrated in Jan–Feb–Mar. Royal Varuna hosts and co-hosts:

  • ILCA (Laser-class) regattas
  • OK Dinghy Asian/world events
  • Optimist (junior) class regattas
  • Dart + multihull catamaran events
  • Hobie Fleet racing
  • Multihull Fleet events
  • Top of the Gulf Regatta (one of Asia's headline annual events at sister venue Ocean Marina, with Royal Varuna participation)
For sailing tourists, attending a regatta as a spectator costs nothing — drive to the club, watch the racing, eat at the beach bar. Major events include public-facing programming with live commentary.

Membership and access

Membership is application-based, with sponsorship from existing members typical. Annual fees + initiation costs apply. The club retains the British members-club ethos — formal-ish, dress-code-aware, but warm and welcoming once you're in.

Reciprocal membership is honoured with most major yacht clubs globally. If you belong to RORC, NYYC, Royal Hong Kong Yacht Club, RYA Yachtmaster, or similar — check with Royal Varuna's secretary whether your home club's reciprocal applies.

Non-member access routes:

  • Booking an RYA training course (open to public)
  • Attending major regattas as a spectator
  • Being invited as a guest of a member
  • Applying for membership

How to visit

For RYA training courses: book directly via varuna.org with your dates and chosen certification level. The club coordinates instructors, boats, and schedule. Bring sailing-appropriate clothing (quick-dry, sun protection, deck shoes).

For regatta attendance: check the calendar at varuna.org/calendar for upcoming events — most are open to spectators with parking and clubhouse access during major weekends. The Jan–Mar window is the most active.

For membership applications: contact the club secretary via the website. Existing-member sponsorship is helpful but not always required for public-application slots.

For travellers planning a Pattaya sailing trip, pair Royal Varuna with Ocean Marina (15 min south at Na Jomtien) for berthing/charter logistics, SailBreeze (also at Ocean Marina) for accessible-tourist sailing intro, and Clubloongchat (Na Jomtien beach) for the multi-discipline kite/wing/wind/SUP scene. The Pattaya sailing ecosystem is dense once you know the four anchor venues.
