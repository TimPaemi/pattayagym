# Fairtex Training Center Pattaya

## Overview

Fairtex is the closest thing Muay Thai has to a globally recognized luxury brand, and its Pattaya facility is the headline destination for international fight tourists. More than just a gym, the Pattaya location is a fully integrated **training resort** — a working camp, a 3-star hotel, a 25-meter swimming pool, a fully equipped weights room, and one of the most decorated stables of fighters in the sport, all on one campus. It draws everyone from total first-timers on a one-week holiday to active world champions doing fight camps.

If you want to walk into one place and have your entire training-and-life logistics solved — bed, bag, breakfast, two-a-days with elite trainers, no taxi rides — Fairtex Pattaya is built for that.

## History

The Fairtex story starts in 1958 when **Bunjong Busarakamwongs**, better known as **Philip Wong**, started selling Muay Thai equipment in Bangkok. The Fairtex Garments factory was incorporated in 1971 and the first Fairtex Gym opened in **Bangkok in 1975**. For three decades the camp produced Lumpinee, Rajadamnern, and World champions out of the capital.

In **2005**, Wong relocated the gym and his entire fight stable from Bangkok to Pattaya, building a purpose-designed training resort. His son, **Prem Busarabavonwongs**, runs the Pattaya operation today. The brand is now active in equipment, apparel, gyms (Pattaya, Bangplee, San Francisco, Singapore), and fighter management — but the Pattaya campus is widely considered the spiritual home.

## Notable Alumni

Fairtex's stable reads like a who's who of modern Muay Thai and MMA:

- **Yodsanklai "The Boxing Computer" Fairtex** — World champion, ONE Championship star, one of the most-watched Muay Thai fighters of the 2010s
- **Saemapetch Fairtex** — ONE Championship Muay Thai world title contender
- **Stamp Fairtex** — ONE Atomweight Muay Thai, Kickboxing, and MMA champion (the only fighter to hold three-sport ONE titles)
- **Jongsanan "The Woodenman" Fairtex** — Double Lumpinee champion, ISKA world champion
- **Boonkerd Fairtex** — Lumpinee champion
- **Nuengsiam Fairtex** — Lumpinee champion
- **Worawut Fairtex** — Toyota champion
- **Fahsritong Fairtex** — Radja champion
- **Denice "The Menace" Zamboanga** — ONE Championship MMA contender
- **Mark "Tyson" Fairtex Abelardo** — ONE Championship MMA

Trainer **Yak** was for many years Yodsanklai's primary coach.

## Trainers

The trainer roster is large (typically 15-25 active pad-holders) and built from former pro Thai fighters. Sessions are heavily individualized — students are paired one-on-one for pads, and trainers rotate so you experience different styles. The senior coaching staff has corner experience in Lumpinee, Rajadamnern, and ONE Championship.

## Daily Schedule

Two sessions a day, six days a week (closed Sundays):

- **Morning session:** ~07:30-10:30 — warm-up run, shadow boxing, padwork (one-on-one), bagwork, clinching, conditioning
- **Afternoon session:** ~15:30-18:30 — same structure, with sparring days mid-week

Each padwork round is **5 rounds × 4 minutes** with a senior trainer. Beginners get scaled progression; advanced/fighter-track students get sparring and clinch-heavy days.

## Pricing (2026)

Posted on the official site:

- **Muay Thai only (1 month, 1 session/day):** ~฿12,000
- **All-Inclusive (Muay Thai + MMA + BJJ, 1 month):** ~฿15,000
- **Muay Thai + BJJ Unlimited (1 month):** ~฿19,500 (drops to ~฿17,500/mo on 6-month commit)
- **Drop-ins / weekly packages:** available — check current rates on the site or at reception

Multi-month commitments unlock material discounts. Combined training-and-accommodation packages are the standard sell — ~฿28-50/night room rate for the on-site barracks/hotel layered on top.

## Facilities

- Multiple Muay Thai rings (regulation size)
- 60+ heavy bags, banana bags, uppercut bags, body bags
- Dedicated MMA cage and BJJ mat area
- Full weight room with cardio (treadmills, bikes, ergs), kettlebells, free weights
- **25-meter outdoor swimming pool**
- Sauna and recovery facilities
- Pro shop on-site (Fairtex gear at source)
- Restaurant / café with healthy + Thai menu

## Accommodation

Three on-campus options:

- **The Barracks** — basic but solid AC rooms, dedicated to fighters/students. Cheapest option, communal vibe. The trade-off: no-frills, but you're 30 seconds from the ring.
- **Fairtex Sports Club & Hotel** — 3-star hotel rooms with breakfast buffet, ~฿1,400-1,700/night. Family/comfort tier.
- **Fairtex Residence** — apartment-style for longer stays.

Reviews repeatedly highlight the **staff friendliness** and the convenience of having pool, gym, food, training, and bed in one campus.

## Location & Getting There

- **Area:** Naklua / North Pattaya, ~5 km north of Pattaya Beach Road central
- **Nearby:** Terminal 21 mall (~2 km), Wong Amat Beach (~1.5 km), Sanctuary of Truth (~3 km)
- Easy taxi/Bolt/Grab from anywhere in Pattaya. Free parking on-site.

## Pros

- Elite trainer pedigree, world-champion lineage
- One-stop campus (train, eat, sleep, swim)
- Great for first-time Muay Thai travelers — language and logistics handled
- Pro shop on-site for gear
- Strong English support, easy booking from abroad
- Reliable WiFi, breakfast, and recovery facilities

## Cons

- Premium pricing relative to traditional Thai camps
- "Gym tourism" feel during peak season — can be crowded
- Sessions are large; less one-on-one face time with senior trainers vs. smaller camps
- Hotel rooms rated as "basic" by some despite the price tier

## Reputation Summary

Universally regarded as one of the **top 3 Muay Thai destinations in Thailand** for foreign students, alongside Tiger Muay Thai (Phuket) and Sityodtong (also Pattaya). Particularly strong reputation for women fighters, beginners on training holidays, and serious athletes wanting access to championship-grade trainers without needing a Thai-language bridge.

## Best For

Beginners on a 1-2 week holiday • Returning amateur fighters doing fight camps • Travelers who want hotel-level comfort with their training • Foreigners who don't speak Thai

## Not Best For

Pure budget travelers (cheaper authentic camps exist) • Students looking for a tiny family-run village vibe • Pros looking to avoid tourism crowds
