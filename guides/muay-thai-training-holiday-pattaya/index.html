Muay Thai training holiday.

By Tim Paemi · 6 min read · Updated 2026-06-02 · How we rank →

Fly in to train twice a day, recover, repeat — Pattaya has been processing foreign training holidays since the 1980s with camps from bunkhouse to full resort.

Pattaya · 157 venues hand-checked · Compare gyms

A Muay Thai training holiday in Pattaya means you fly in with one job: train twice a day, recover, repeat — without spending half your trip on logistics. Pattaya is not Bangkok (lineage museums) or Phuket (resort marketing). It is the city where foreigners have trained at scale since the 1980s: English-friendly kru, fight nights most weeks, and packages from budget bunkhouse to full resort.

This guide is for the 1–4 week traveller (and the 1–3 month serious amateur) who wants a realistic plan: which camps run real holidays, what you will pay, how days are structured, and what to book before you land.

Why Pattaya for a training holiday (not only Phuket)

  • Density: 19 verified Muay Thai venues in one city — switch camps mid-trip if coach fit is wrong.
  • Cost: Stay-and-train packages often run 20–40% below Phuket resort camps for comparable hours.
  • English: Higher share of Tier-A English kru than most Thai provinces outside Bangkok.
  • Fight ecosystem: MAX Muay Thai cards and Bangkok stadiums within a day trip.
  • Long-stay life: visa planning, food, remote-work cafés.

Training holiday lengths — what changes

1 week (introduction)

Goal: stance, basic combos, pad rhythm, gym culture. One session per day is enough. Pick beginner-friendly camps. Budget ฿15,000–35,000 all-in with accommodation.

2 weeks (skill block)

Goal: consistent improvement, maybe light sparring. Two-a-days optional. Sweet spot for first fight tourists. Budget ฿25,000–55,000.

3–4 weeks (holiday that might become relocation)

Goal: conditioning, possible amateur prep. On-site accommodation (stay-and-train guide) or apartment near gym. Budget ฿40,000–120,000+ premium all-inclusive.

Recovery and non-training days

Schedule one rest day per week in week one, two in week three if soreness stacks. Pool swim (swimming guide), yoga (yoga retreat), or beach walk — not full-day drinking before morning pads. Massage is cheap and good; avoid deep tissue hours before hard sparring.

Visa and long-stay notes

Most holidays run on tourist visa or exemption. Multi-month: education pathways — training & visa guide and Pattaya Visa Help, not gym sales desk alone.

FAQ

How much does a Muay Thai training holiday in Pattaya cost?

Budget once-daily + own hotel: ฿8,000–15,000/week training. All-inclusive resort: ฿20,000–55,000/week.

Is Pattaya or Phuket better for a Muay Thai holiday?

Phuket markets resort packages; Pattaya offers more camp choice and lower average cost. Compare: Pattaya vs Phuket.

Do I need experience before a training holiday?

No. Tell camps your level when booking — fundamentals pads, not forced sparring.

Can I train Muay Thai in Pattaya for one month?

Yes. Monthly training ฿4,000–12,000 mid-tier; resort packages higher. Accommodation guide.

What should I pack?

Hand wraps, mouthguard, optional shin guards week 1, running shoes, many shirts. Gloves often rentable. Buy Thai shorts locally ฿300–600.

Nutrition on a training holiday

Fighters underestimate calories. Two-a-days in heat burn aggressively. Camp meals are often carb-heavy — fine for training load. Add protein shake or eggs if you lift too. Hydration: 3–4 litres water daily minimum; electrolyte sachets from 7-Eleven are cheap insurance. Alcohol destroys next-morning pads — save drinks for post-camp rest days.

Gear shop run (first 48 hours)

Buy Thai shorts locally, hand wraps, mouthguard if you did not pack. Shin guards before hard sparring weeks. Running shoes for road work — verify camp road-run policy. Some camps sell gloves; sizes may be limited — bring your own if 16oz+ is mandatory for sparring.

Fight spectating without fighting

Holiday does not require amateur bout. Watching MAX teaches rhythm and respect. Bangkok cards via day-trip guide are pilgrimage-tier — book transport, not just tickets, from Pattaya hotels.

When to extend vs fly home

Rule of thumb: if you still want morning pads on day 6 of a 7-day trip, extend. If ankles and shins scream through stairs, take rest days instead of extending blindly. Month-two athletes often relocate housing to Jomtien while keeping the same camp commute — cheaper than resort weekly rates.

Group holidays and friends with different goals

One friend wants hard sparring, another wants tourist photos — split mornings (different camps) or pick Fairtex/Kombat where both coexist. Non-training friends should not book rural east camps without their own transport entertainment plan. Agree on alcohol rules before the trip — one hungover friend delays everyone's morning session.

Corporate retreat style groups: ask camps for private ring blocks and team-building pad rounds — Fairtex and large resorts handle this routinely. Invoice VAT needs for Thai companies: ask finance desk, not front-line trainer.

Share goals in writing before arrival — weight loss, technique, or fun — coaches assign different pad holders. Vague "get fit" requests produce vague sessions.

Jet lag: skip pads on arrival day if you landed red-eye — walk, swim, sleep; start proper camp rhythm day two to avoid preventable shin splits from tired technique.
