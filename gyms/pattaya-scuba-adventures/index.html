Overview

Pattaya Scuba Adventures is a PADI dive center serving the Pattaya near-island reef cluster — primarily Koh Larn (Coral Island), Koh Sak, and Koh Krok, all within a 30-minute boat ride off the Pattaya coast. The center caters to the wide spectrum of recreational divers: from first-time bubble-blowers booking a Discover Scuba Diving experience to certified open-water divers wanting fun-dive day trips on Pattaya's most accessible reefs.

Pattaya's near-island diving offers a sheltered, beginner-friendly entry point to Thai scuba diving — with moderate visibility, calm currents, and reefs at recreational depths (5-18m) — making it different in character from the deeper, more current-driven, more famous Andaman Sea sites (Phuket, Similan Islands, Koh Tao). Pattaya near-island diving is not where you go for record-setting visibility or whale shark encounters — it's where you go for convenient, repeatable, training-ready diving from a base where you can also enjoy nightlife, shopping, and beach life.

For travellers who want to try scuba in Pattaya or maintain dive certification with regular fun dives, Pattaya Scuba Adventures provides accessible, mid-tier service at the standard PADI dive shop quality level.

Pattaya Near Islands — The Dive Region

The Pattaya near-island cluster sits 6 km off the Pattaya coast — accessible by 30-minute boat ride from Bali Hai Pier. The three main dive islands:

Koh Larn (Coral Island)

  • Largest of the near islands — a popular tourist day-trip destination above water
  • Multiple dive sites around the island shores
  • Soft and hard coral gardens
  • Marine life observed:
  • Nudibranchs — dozens of species including chromodoris, phyllidiella, hypselodoris
  • Moray eels — green moray, white-eyed moray
  • Bamboo sharks resting under ledges (small, harmless)
  • Octopus hiding in crevices
  • Schools of damselfish, butterflyfish, parrotfish
  • Occasional rays in sandy patches
  • Depths: 5-18m typical
  • Suitable for: Open Water through any certification level

Koh Sak

  • Smaller island between Koh Larn and the mainland
  • Resident hawksbill sea turtles — often spotted on dives
  • Fish life:
  • Parrotfish, blue-spotted stingrays
  • Lionfish — multiple species
  • Damselfish schools weaving through branching corals
  • Coral:
  • Branching corals (acropora)
  • Soft corals
  • Anemones with clownfish
  • Suitable for: Beginner-friendly with sheltered conditions

Koh Krok

  • Nearest island to Pattaya — only 9 km offshore
  • Long, finger-shaped islet
  • Sloping reef from 5m down to ~12m
  • Highlights:
  • Huge anemone garden at the south end — full of clownfish
  • Hard corals and sea fans
  • Plethora of reef fish
  • Suitable for: Open Water students and casual fun divers

What Pattaya Scuba Adventures Offers

Discover Scuba Diving (DSD) — No Certification Needed

  • Single-session intro to scuba — no swim test, no prior experience
  • Pool / shallow water briefing
  • Two ocean dives at 5-12m typically
  • Full equipment provided
  • PADI DSD certification card (not full certification but proof of experience)
  • Cost: ~฿3,500-4,500 typical

PADI Open Water Diver Course

  • 3-4 day program
  • Full PADI certification valid worldwide
  • 5 training dives progressing from confined to open water
  • Theory study (e-Learning available)
  • Final certification to 18m recreational depth
  • Cost: ~฿14,000-17,000 typical

PADI Advanced Open Water

  • 2-day course for already-certified divers
  • 5 specialty dives including deep, navigation, and 3 electives
  • Certification to 30m depth
  • Cost: ~฿11,000-13,000 typical

PADI Specialty Courses

  • Deep Diver, Wreck, Navigation, Nitrox, Photography, etc.
  • Single-specialty 1-2 day courses
  • Five specialties + 50 logged dives = Master Scuba Diver (recreational topcap)

Fun Dives for Certified Divers

  • 2-tank boat trip: ~฿2,000-2,500 typical
  • Includes equipment, lunch, dive briefing, boat fees
  • Multi-dive packages discount per-dive cost
  • Departures most days — confirm via website

Boat Operations

The dive operation runs from Bali Hai Pier (south end of Pattaya Beach) — Pattaya's main pier for offshore departures. Boats:

  • Wood-hull or fiberglass dive boats — sized for 8-25 divers
  • Sun deck + shaded area
  • Tank racks for gear storage during travel
  • Restroom on board
  • Snacks, water, lunch on full-day trips
  • Quick reboarding ladders

Equipment

  • Full rental gear — BCDs, regulators, fins, masks, snorkels, exposure suits
  • Tanks (aluminum 12L typical)
  • Weights (lead + integrated weight options)
  • Dive computers typically rentable

Bring your own mask if possible (fit is critical) and consider a personal dive computer for serious diving.

  • Sheltered near-island diving — beginner-friendly conditions
  • Full PADI certification programs
  • Reef + small-shark + turtle diving at modest depths
  • 30-minute boat ride vs. multi-hour Andaman Sea departures
  • Cost-effective vs. Phuket / Koh Tao for the same certification
  • Flexible scheduling — daily departures
  • English communication

Cons

✕
  • Visibility lower than Andaman Sea sites (3-15m typical vs. 15-30m+)
  • Pattaya Bay water quality — some cloudiness, periodic algal influence
  • No big pelagics — no whale sharks, manta rays in Pattaya
  • Recreational depths only for near islands
  • Best advanced diving requires longer boat rides to far islands or even other Thai regions
  • Standard recreational tier — not the highest-credentialed Pattaya operator (No Limit Divers / Mermaid's are PADI 5-Star IDC / CDC respectively)

Best For

★

  • Open Water certification during a Pattaya vacation
  • Refresher dives for already-certified divers staying in Pattaya
  • Families and groups wanting accessible introduction
  • Budget-conscious certification seekers vs. Phuket/Koh Tao costs

  • Crystal-visibility chasers — Pattaya can't deliver Andaman-grade clarity
