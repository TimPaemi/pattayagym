# Laem Chabang International Country Club

## Overview

Laem Chabang International Country Club is **the most highly regarded golf course in the Pattaya region** and one of the most respected championship courses in Thailand. A **27-hole Jack Nicklaus design** opened in **1995**, it sprawls across **700 acres** of dramatic terrain in Sriracha district, Chonburi, **30 minutes north of central Pattaya** and **109 kilometres southeast of Bangkok**. The course is one of **only six Nicklaus designs in Thailand**, all completed between 1991 and 1994, and is the only one widely considered the **best layout in the Pattaya golf region**.

The course has been **nominated for Best Course in Thailand every single year of the World Golf Awards** — a level of consistent recognition matched by very few Asian courses. For golfers traveling to Pattaya — a region with **30+ courses within an hour of central Pattaya** — Laem Chabang is the **default top-of-the-list pick** for serious players who want a championship-caliber Nicklaus experience.

## The Designer — Jack Nicklaus

Few designers carry more weight in golf course architecture than **Jack Nicklaus** — the "Golden Bear", winner of **18 major championships** (the all-time men's record), and one of the most prolific course designers in modern golf. Nicklaus Design has produced over **400 courses worldwide**, with a recognizable architectural philosophy emphasizing:

- **Natural integration with terrain** — fairways follow existing land contours
- **Strategic risk/reward angles** — multiple playable lines for different abilities
- **Heavy bunkering** — well-placed sand traps as primary defenses
- **Water-feature drama** — lakes, streams, creeks integrated into hole design
- **Pristine maintenance standards** — Nicklaus courses are known for premium conditioning

Laem Chabang was designed during what is considered Nicklaus's **peak Asian-design period in the early 1990s**, when he was simultaneously developing six Thai courses. The course represents one of the strongest expressions of his philosophy in Southeast Asia.

## The Three Nines — Lake, Mountain, Valley

Laem Chabang is structured as **three distinct 9-hole courses** that can be combined into any 18-hole round. Each nine has its own character driven by the terrain it occupies:

### Mountain 9 — 3,446 yards

Routes around **mountain-style hills** with significant elevation change. The course makes the most of the natural topography, with elevated tee shots and strategic doglegs around hillsides. Generally considered the **most dramatic** of the three nines, with the strongest visual impact and the most demanding tee-shot decisions.

### Lake 9 — 3,419 yards

Built around a **stream that runs through multiple holes**, plus larger lakes and water features. Water in play on most holes — strategic water-carry decisions and forced layups define the playing experience. Generally considered the **most demanding** for shot precision.

### Valley 9 — 3,619 yards (the longest)

Routes through valleys **surrounded by picturesque mountains**. The longest of the three nines, offering more length-driven holes and broader fairways. Often considered the **most playable** for a wider range of abilities while still presenting championship-quality challenge.

### Recommended Combinations

The **Mountain + Valley** (also known as **B&C** combination) is featured on Top 100 Golf Courses lists as Laem Chabang's signature 18-hole experience. Lake + Valley is also commonly played; Lake + Mountain offers the most varied terrain experience.

**Caddies are compulsory on all Pattaya golf courses, including Laem Chabang.**

- **Caddy fee:** ฿450 (typically included in golf packages)
- **Caddy tip (separate):** ~฿300 recommended after the round, paid directly to the caddy
- **Caddy quality at Laem Chabang** is **ranked top-tier** — knowledgeable about reads, distances, course strategy
- Caddies handle clubs, distance reading, line-of-play guidance, and ball-finding

## Reputation Summary

Laem Chabang International Country Club is **the consensus best golf course in Pattaya region** and **one of the top championship courses in all of Thailand**. The combination of Nicklaus design pedigree, 27-hole layout flexibility, pristine course conditioning, top-tier caddies, and immaculate clubhouse infrastructure puts it in a category of its own among Pattaya's golf options.

For the **golf-focused traveller with one round to play in Pattaya, this is the choice.** For multi-day golf holidays, Laem Chabang typically anchors the itinerary — usually paired with **Siam Country Club Old Course** (the other Pattaya marquee), and supplemented with mid-tier rounds at **Burapha** or **Phoenix Gold** depending on preference.
